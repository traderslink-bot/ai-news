Summary

Press release: Navitas (NASDAQ: NVTS) reported Q4 2025 revenue of $7.3M (down from $10.1M in Q3 and $18.0M YoY). GAAP loss from operations was $41.4M in Q4 (includes $16.6M restructuring and impairment charge); non-GAAP loss from operations was $12.1M. Cash and cash equivalents were $236.9M at year-end, reflecting a November 2025 private placement that generated $95.6M net proceeds. The company says high-power markets (AI data centers, grid/energy infrastructure, performance computing, industrial electrification) represented a majority of quarterly revenue for the first time and mobile declined to under 25% of revenue. Navitas announced a long-term GaN foundry and technology partnership with GlobalFoundries, strategic partnerships with Cyient Semiconductors and distribution deals with WT Microelectronics and Avnet, and accelerated customer sampling of new 650V and 100V GaN and 2300V/3300V SiC products. Q1 2026 guidance: revenue $8.0M–$8.5M, non-GAAP gross margin ~38.7% ±25 bps, and non-GAAP operating expenses ≈ $15M. Company expects sequential top-line growth starting Q1 2026 driven by high-power markets.
