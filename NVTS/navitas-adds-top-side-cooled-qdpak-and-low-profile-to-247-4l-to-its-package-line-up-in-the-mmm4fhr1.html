Summary

Press release: Navitas Semiconductor (NASDAQ: NVTS) launched two new 1200 V SiC MOSFET packages — a top-side cooled QDPAK and a low-profile TO-247-4L — built on its 5th-generation Trench-Assisted Planar (TAP) GeneSiC technology. The TAP platform delivers ~35% improvement in RDS,ON × QGD figure-of-merit and ~25% improvement in QGD/QGS ratio, with VGS,TH > 3 V to reduce parasitic turn-on. The QDPAK (15 mm × 21 mm, 2.3 mm height) enables top-side heatsinking, lower parasitic inductance, larger die sizes and higher current capability; the TO-247-4-LP reduces vertical PCB height and uses asymmetrical leads for tighter manufacturing tolerances, targeting AI data center and high-density power applications. Published part numbers and RDS,ON values include G5R06MT12QP (QDPAK, 6.5 mΩ), G5R12MT12QP (QDPAK, 12 mΩ), G5R06MT12LK (TO-247-4-LP, 6.5 mΩ) and G5R12MT12LK (TO-247-4-LP, 12 mΩ). The release references a downloadable white paper and reiterates standard forward-looking risk disclosures.
