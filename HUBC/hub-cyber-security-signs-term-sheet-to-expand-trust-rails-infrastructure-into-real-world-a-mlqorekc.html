Summary

HUB Cyber Security Ltd. (NASDAQ: HUBC) announced a term sheet to acquire 100% of Ferrox Critical Minerals Ltd. and its Tivani minerals project in South Africa. Tivani reportedly contains ~519 million tons of titaniferous magnetite (NI 43-101 compliant), is fully permitted with secured mining rights, and is positioned to commence production, processing and sales in FY2026. The proposed transaction contemplates $125 million in equity consideration issued by HUB in four stages; upon execution of definitive agreements HUB would issue 19.9% of its outstanding shares in the first stage, with additional share issuances tied to three project performance milestones and subject to shareholder approval, due diligence, regulatory approvals and customary conditions. HUB positions the asset as a revenue-bearing anchor for its Trust Rails platform to extend its secured data/validation infrastructure into real-world asset validation. The release includes a detailed risk/forward-looking statement list.

Positives
  • Tivani project reported to hold ~519 million tons of titaniferous magnetite with NI 43-101 compliant resources
  • Asset is described as fully permitted with secured mining rights, environmental approvals, water licenses, rail and port connectivity
  • More than US$70 million invested in the Tivani project to date
  • Company expects Tivani to provide titanium dioxide, iron and vanadium feedstock relevant to aerospace, defense and energy transition supply chains
  • Transaction is structured to integrate a physical, revenue-bearing asset with HUB's Trust Rails digital validation platform
Negatives
  • Transaction proposes issuance of $125 million in HUB equity, including an initial 19.9% of outstanding shares — significant potential dilution
  • Transaction is subject to due diligence, definitive agreements, regulatory approvals and HUB shareholder approval; no assurance of closing
  • Project and commercialization risks noted (permits, construction, financing, commodity price volatility, infrastructure and operational risks)
  • HUB discloses existing risks including uncertainty regarding liquidity, continued Nasdaq listing standards, limited liquidity/trading of its securities
