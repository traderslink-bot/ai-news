Summary

Star Equity Fund, a 5.4% holder of GEE Group (NYSE: JOB), urged the Board to hire an independent investment bank to run a competitive sale process, report to the Board's M&A Committee, and pursue a sale to the highest bidder to maximize stockholder value. The fund criticized the Board's past strategic review and highlighted deteriorating financial performance and a large decline in the stock price, while stating readiness to engage constructively with the Board.

Positives
  • Advocates for an independent, banker-led sale process to maximize stockholder value.
  • Calls for hiring a reputable investment bank with experience in staffing and professional services to run the sale process.
  • Bank should report directly to the Board's M&A Committee to avoid conflicts of interest.
  • Supports a real, competitive sale process that broadly markets the company and evaluates all credible proposals.
  • Expresses willingness to engage constructively with the Board to maximize value.
Negatives
  • Board's 2023–2024 strategic review is described as failed and as endorsing the status quo rather than pursuing a sale or transformative transaction.
  • GEE Group's revenue in FY2025 was $96.5 million, down 42% from FY2022 peak and down 10% vs FY2024.
  • Shares have traded close to cash per share since March 2025 and the stock price declined about 86% over the last five years.
