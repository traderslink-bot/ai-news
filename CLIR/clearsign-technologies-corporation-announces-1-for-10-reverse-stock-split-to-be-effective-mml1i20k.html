Summary

ClearSign Technologies (NASDAQ: CLIR) announced a 1-for-10 reverse stock split effective 12:01 a.m. ET on March 16, 2026. The company expects its common stock to trade on a split-adjusted basis under the same ticker CLIR on Nasdaq with new CUSIP 185064201. The split, approved by stockholders on February 26, 2026, reduces outstanding shares from approximately 54.1 million to approximately 5.41 million while leaving authorized shares and par value unchanged. No fractional shares will be issued; DTC participants will be rounded up to whole shares at the participant level. Outstanding warrants and equity awards (and their exercise prices) will be proportionately adjusted. The transfer agent VStock Transfer, LLC is acting as exchange agent. The company states the reverse split is intended to increase per-share trading price to regain compliance with Nasdaq's $1.00 minimum bid requirement. A Form 8-K relating to the reverse split will be filed on or about March 11, 2026.
