Summary

Press release: Lumen Technologies (NYSE: LUMN) declared completion of its turnaround at its 2026 Investor Day and outlined a multi-year growth plan focused on becoming an enterprise-focused, AI-ready network provider. Key points: nearly $13 billion in Private Connectivity Fabric (PCF) deals to date (including an Anthropic contract); deployed 17 million intercity fiber miles (targeting ~47M by 2028 and ~58M by 2031); Network-as-a-Service platform exceeding 2,000 customers; rollout of Multi-Cloud Gateway; sale of Mass Markets fiber business to AT&T for $5.75B and debt reduced to under $13B; roughly $500M annual interest savings and ~$1B reduced capex; ratings upgrades by all three major agencies; financial targets include expanding adjusted EBITDA margin from 27.1% (2025) to mid-30% range by 2030 and business segment revenue growth by 2028. The company will change revenue reporting to products and services starting Q1 2026. The release contains forward-looking statements and associated risks.
