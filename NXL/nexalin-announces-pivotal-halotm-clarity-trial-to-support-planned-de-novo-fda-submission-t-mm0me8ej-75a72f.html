Summary

Nexalin Technology announces a pivotal HALO Clarity trial to support a planned de novo FDA submission for a drug-free insomnia treatment. The study is randomized, triple-blinded, sham-controlled with at least 150 participants across the U.S., conducted in collaboration with Lindus Health, and conducted as a fully decentralized trial via the NeuroCare virtual clinic. Participants will receive four weeks of active HALO Clarity therapy or sham treatment, followed by four weeks of follow-up. The trial leverages prior Nexalin data and international regulatory approvals, and aims to support FDA clearance for the DIFS-based insomnia treatment in a large, high-prevalence market estimated at about 30 million U.S. adults. Nexalin’s Gen-2 15 mA device has international approvals for insomnia. Risks include typical forward-looking statements uncertainties and the possibility that trial enrollment, results, or regulatory success may not meet expectations.

Positives
  • Randomized, triple-blinded, sham-controlled design with a minimum of 150 participants.
  • Fully decentralized trial using the NeuroCare virtual clinic, expanding access and reducing site visit requirements.
  • Collaboration with Lindus Health to manage protocol finalization, regulatory prep, patient recruitment, data oversight, and submission support.
  • Builds on prior Nexalin clinical results and international regulatory approvals, including a previously published 120-participant study showing improvements over placebo.
  • Gen-2 15 mA device already has international regulatory approvals for insomnia-related indications.
  • Targets a de novo FDA submission for a non-pharmacological, drug-free insomnia treatment, addressing a large and underserved market.
  • Insomnia affects an estimated 30 million adults in the United States; potential global multi-billion-dollar market.
Negatives
  • No guarantee of FDA clearance or successful de novo submission.
  • Trial enrollment, execution, and regulatory outcomes are uncertain and subject to risk.
  • Dependence on third-party collaborator Lindus Health for key trial activities and regulatory preparation.
  • Forward-looking statements risk factors noted; results may differ from expectations.
  • International approvals do not guarantee U.S. regulatory success for the same device or indication.
