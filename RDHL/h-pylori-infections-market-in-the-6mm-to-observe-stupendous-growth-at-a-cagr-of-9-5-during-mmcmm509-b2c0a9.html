Summary

Forecast of growth in the H. pylori infections market for the 6 major markets (US, EU4, UK) with a projected CAGR of 9.5% for 2026–2036. 2025 market size cited at USD 270 million, with the US accounting for about 85% of 2025 revenue. Growth driven by shift to optimized combination regimens (triple- and quadruple-drug therapies), rising diagnosed prevalence, use of RT-PCR for tailored therapy, and ongoing development of emerging therapies including Rifasutenizol, Linaprazan glurate, and Tegoprazan. Regulatory updates and pipeline activity noted, with TALICIA and VOQUEZNA among near-term options. The pipeline is described as limited.

Positives
  • CAGR forecast of 9.5% for 2026–2036 in the 6MM market.
  • 2025 market size in the 6MM is USD 270 million.
  • US accounted for about 85% of 2025 market size.
  • Total diagnosed prevalent cases in the 6MM were around 147 million in 2025, with projected growth to 2036.
  • Emerging therapies such as Rifasutenizol, Linaprazan glurate, and Tegoprazan are in development and could impact the market.
  • RT-PCR and other molecular diagnostics support tailored H. pylori treatment.
  • Addition of bismuth to triple therapy improves eradication rates (30–40% in resistant strains).
  • Regulatory progress: UK MAA for TALICIA; NMPA acceptance of rifasutenizol NDA in China; FDA/EMA activity include pediatric study waiver for linaprazan glurate; Phase III data for rifasutenizol reported >90% eradication.
  • Phase III results for Rifasutenizol show high eradication rates in first-line use.
Negatives
  • Concomitant quadruple therapy increases antibiotic exposure and raises antimicrobial stewardship concerns.
  • Vonoprazan-based VOQUEZNA products have limited regulatory availability in Western markets (not approved in EU4 or UK).
  • Rifabutin-based TALICIA approved in the US but not yet approved by EMA; UK may be first European market to authorize.
  • Emerging therapy pipeline in H. pylori infections is described as limited with only a few agents in development.
  • Traditional regimens with high pill burden may affect patient adherence.
