Summary

This document is a Form 6-K filed by RedHill Biopharma Ltd. reporting an attached press release that the full commercial launch of Talicia in the U.S. has commenced under a joint commercialization agreement. Talicia Holdings Inc. (THI), a jointly controlled operating entity, will manage launch and ongoing commercialization; THI is owned 70% by RedHill and 30% by Cumberland Pharmaceuticals (Cumberland made a $4.0 million strategic investment). The filing describes active commercial intent (joint co-commercialization) to accelerate prescriptions and market penetration for Talicia — an FDA-approved, all-in-one, low-dose rifabutin-based H. pylori therapy listed as a first-line option in the 2024 ACG guideline, with U.S. patent protection through 2042 and eight years of QIDP exclusivity. The release reiterates market opportunity (H. pylori affects ~35% of U.S. adults; ~1.6M treated annually), plans to expand access in the UK and Middle East, and standard forward-looking risk disclosures (including that the co-commercialization may not yield anticipated benefits and other operational, regulatory and market risks).
