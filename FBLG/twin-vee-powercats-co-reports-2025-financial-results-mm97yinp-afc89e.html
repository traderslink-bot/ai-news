Summary

Twin Vee PowerCats Co. reported 2025 results with net sales of $14,819,130, gross income of $1,257,105, and a net loss of $8,607,273. The company completed manufacturing modernization, reduced field inventory, and plans to relaunch Bahama Boat Works in March 2026; launched Black Line Defense and expanded its dealer network, with management citing improved margins and revenue momentum.

Positives
  • Return to year-over-year revenue growth in the second half of 2025
  • 60% year-over-year net sales growth in Q4 2025
  • Gross margin improved to 8.5%
  • Loss from operations reduced year over year
  • Net loss reduced by 39% year over year
  • Field inventory reduced from approximately 140 units at peak in 2024 to 52 units at year-end 2025
  • Manufacturing campus modernization increased production space to approximately 100,000 square feet
  • In-house tooling with a five-axis CNC router reduces development costs by approximately 33%
  • Relaunch of Bahama Boat Works planned for March 2026
  • Acquisition of Bahama Boat Works assets completed in June 2025
  • Launched Black Line Defense for homeland security and maritime applications
Negatives
  • Net loss of $8,607,273 for 2025
  • Loss from operations of $8,781,299
  • Operating expenses of $10,038,404
  • Adjusted net loss of $4,939,687 (vs. $9,290,962 in 2024)
