Summary

Caliber (NASDAQ: CWD) announced the sale of the Holiday Inn Ocotillo in the Phoenix–Chandler submarket for $13.0 million. The asset was owned by Caliber Hospitality Trust, Inc. (CHT). Proceeds will be recycled to fund growth, combined with new equity raised through Caliber's in-house capital markets platform and institutional partnerships, to expand the CHT portfolio in 2026–2027. CHT is designed to aggregate branded, cash-flowing hotels and may pursue liquidity pathways, including a non-traded REIT structure, with the long-term objective of a public listing. The announcement notes sector-wide capital-structure challenges and mentions that some opportunities were not pursued if return thresholds were not met.

Positives
  • Sale of Holiday Inn Ocotillo completed for $13.0 million.
  • Proceeds to be recycled and used to fund growth and expansion of Caliber Hospitality Trust (CHT) in 2026–2027.
  • CHT strategy focuses on aggregating branded, cash-flowing hotels through tax-efficient transactions and value-add opportunities.
  • Caliber intends to raise additional equity through its in-house capital markets platform and institutional partnerships.
  • CHT contemplates flexible liquidity options, including a potential non-traded REIT with quarterly liquidity and a long-term objective of a public listing.
  • Portfolio expansion is expected to increase recurring revenue and operating leverage at Caliber.
Negatives
  • Capital structures across the hospitality sector remain challenged.
  • Many hotels face upcoming loan maturities and required brand-improvement plans, which can affect profitability.
  • Some opportunities did not close previously because they did not meet return thresholds.
