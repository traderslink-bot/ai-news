Summary

Allurion Technologies, Inc. (NYSE: ALUR) announced that the U.S. Food and Drug Administration has approved the premarket approval (PMA) application for the Allurion Gastric Balloon System featuring the Allurion Smart Capsule. The device is indicated to promote short-term limited weight loss in adults ages 22–65 with BMI ≥30 and ≤40 kg/m2 who have had at least one unsuccessful weight-loss program. The Smart Capsule is swallowed in an office visit, is filled in the stomach, resides on average ~15.3 weeks (about four months), then passes naturally; up to two balloons may be placed in a 10-month period. Company notes the approval opens access to an estimated ~80 million Americans within the approved BMI range, cites >50 patents and >200,000 patients treated outside the U.S., and positions the product as a non-surgical, repeatable alternative or complement to GLP-1 medications and bariatric surgery. The release includes customary forward-looking statements and identifies commercialization, regulatory and competitive risks.
