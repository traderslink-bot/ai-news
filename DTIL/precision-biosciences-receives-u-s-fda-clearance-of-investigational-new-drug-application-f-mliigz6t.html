Summary

Precision BioSciences (NASDAQ: DTIL) announced the FDA issued a Study May Proceed notification for its IND for PBGENE-DMD, allowing IRB review and clinical site activation for the Phase 1/2 FUNCTION-DMD trial. PBGENE-DMD is a first-in-class in vivo gene editing therapy using an ARCUS-based gene excision approach targeting DMD mutations in exons 45-55 (≈60% of patients) to restore a near full-length dystrophin (~80% of full length). The company plans U.S. site activation in H1 2026, expects initial patient data by year-end 2026, and has received FDA Rare Pediatric Disease and Orphan Drug designations. A virtual investor event is planned in March following the Muscular Dystrophy Association meeting.

Positives
  • FDA Study May Proceed notification received, enabling IRB activities and site activation
  • Therapy targets mutations in exons 45–55, covering ~60% of DMD patients
  • Design aims to restore near full-length dystrophin (~80% of full length) versus smaller micro-dystrophin constructs
  • Has FDA Rare Pediatric Disease and Orphan Drug designations
  • Company provided a near-term operational timeline (site activation in H1 2026; initial data by year-end 2026)
Negatives
  • Program is entering first-in-human Phase 1/2 trials; clinical safety and efficacy are unproven
  • Clinical timelines and outcomes are forward-looking and subject to regulatory and IRB processes
  • Therapy requires immune modulation and monitoring, adding complexity to development and administration
  • Initial efficacy readouts depend on biopsy-based dystrophin expression and limited early patient cohorts
