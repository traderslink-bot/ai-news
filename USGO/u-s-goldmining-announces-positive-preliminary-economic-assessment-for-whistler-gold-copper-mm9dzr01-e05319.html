Summary

U.S. GoldMining's Whistler Gold-Copper Project PEA shows after-tax NPV5% of $2.04 billion and IRR of 33.0%, with an initial payback of 2.1 years at base-case prices of $3,200/oz gold, $4.50/lb copper and $37.50/oz silver. The open-pit project is modeled for 40,000 tonnes per day throughput, with average gold recovery of 88.9% and copper recovery of 77.8%. Initial capital costs are $1.28 billion; sustaining capital is $381.1 million; and All-In Sustaining Costs are $1,046/oz Au (by-product). Life-of-mine production totals 3.59 million ounces of gold-equivalent over 14.6 years, including 2.68 Moz Au, 6.90 Moz Ag and 40.4 million lbs Cu. PEA indicates 14.6-year mine life, 2.2:1 strip ratio, and peak annual AuEq of 382.6 koz in year 3. Resource update includes Indicated 299 Mt at 0.57 g/t AuEq (5.41 Moz AuEq) and Inferred 291 Mt at 0.54 g/t AuEq (4.97 Moz AuEq). The PEA is based on indicated resources and is preliminary; full NI 43-101 technical reports will be filed later. Potential upside includes exploration targets (Island Mountain, Raintree) and infrastructure improvements (West Susitna Access Road).

Positives
  • After-tax NPV5% of $2.04 billion
  • After-tax IRR of 33.0%
  • Payback period of 2.1 years
  • Life-of-mine production of 3.59 million ounces AuEq
  • LOM average annual AuEq production of 245.6 koz
  • AISC of $1,046 per oz Au (by-product basis)
  • Initial capital expenditure of $1.28 billion
  • Nominal process throughput of 40,000 tpd with 88.9% Au and 77.8% Cu recoveries
  • Indicated MRE of 299 Mt and Inferred MRE of 291 Mt for 5.41 Moz and 4.97 Moz AuEq, respectively
Negatives
  • PEA is preliminary and uses indicated resources only (no inferred in the base case)
  • Not a mineral reserve; subject to uncertainties and further study
  • Results depend on commodity price assumptions and may change with new data
  • Requires additional studies (e.g., pre-feasibility) and regulatory approvals
  • Infrastructure and permitting dependencies (West Susitna Access Road) could affect development
