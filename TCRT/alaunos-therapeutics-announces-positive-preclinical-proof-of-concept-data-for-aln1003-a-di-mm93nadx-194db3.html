Summary

Alaunos Therapeutics reported early non-GLP preclinical data from two diet-induced obesity mouse studies of ALN1003, an oral, non-hormonal small-molecule candidate for obesity and MASLD. In high-fat-diet mice, ALN1003 produced dose-dependent weight loss, favorable body composition changes, reduced liver weight, and lowered liver injury enzymes with improvements in glucose and total cholesterol. A second palatability/tolerability study showed dose-dependent reductions in food and water intake and fat loss at higher doses, with the highest dose lowering glucose and HDL-C. The company plans additional preclinical studies, formulation/CMC work, manufacturing refinements, IND-enabling activities, and notes a cash runway into Q2 2026 (~$1.9 million as of 9/30/2025).

Positives
  • Demonstrated dose-dependent body weight loss in a diet-induced obesity mouse model
  • Favorable body composition changes and reduced liver weight
  • Lowered liver injury enzymes ALT, AST, and ALP
  • Improvements in metabolic biomarkers (glucose and total cholesterol)
  • Dose-related reductions in adipose depots and signs of reduced fat in BAT
  • Tolerability generally good with only mild, reversible hypolocomotion observed
  • Non-hormonal approach with potential to differentiate from GLP-1 therapies
  • Plans for IND-enabling studies and manufacturing optimization
Negatives
  • Findings are non-GLP, preclinical mouse data; translational risk to humans
  • Small sample sizes (12 mice per group in Study 1; 6 per group in Study 2)
  • In drinking-water study, reduced water intake may confound attribution of weight loss to drug exposure
  • Two high-dose mice showed mild dehydration during the PK portion
  • Liver enzymes showed no statistically significant change after 18 days in the second study
  • Cash runway depends on additional financing and future study outcomes
