iPower Authorizes $2 Million Share Repurchase Program, First in Company History

iPower (IPW) authorized a $2.0 million share repurchase program — the first repurchase authorization in company history. The authorized amount represents a material portion of the company’s market cap (~40% of a $4.9M MC). Combined with a small float (380k) and elevated short interest (16.9%), the buyback has the potential to materially affect available supply; timing and execution details were not specified.

Positive

  • Management authorization of $2M buyback — signals confidence and provides direct demand support.
  • Buyback size is large relative to market cap (~40%), which can meaningfully reduce outstanding supply if executed.
  • Small float (380k) increases the potential market impact of repurchases.
  • Elevated short interest (16.9%) could amplify moves if buybacks are active.

Negative

  • Authorization is not an immediate purchase — timing, cadence and price limits are unspecified.
  • Buybacks may be executed slowly or not fully, so expected supply reduction may not materialize.
  • High short interest also increases downside risk if news or execution disappoints.
