Summary

NUVVE Holding Corp. said its Japanese subsidiary NUVVE JAPAN K.K. signed a definitive sales agreement for a 2 MW, 8 MWh front-of-the-meter battery energy storage system in Mino City, Gifu Prefecture, valued at about $3.35 million, with operations expected in Q4 2026. The system will use the Nuvve GIVe platform, participate in Japan's Supply and Demand Adjustment Market and the Japan Electric Power Exchange (JEPX), and includes an upfront deposit of about $0.928 million. This marks Nuvve's first grid-scale storage sale in Japan. Separately, the company announced a partnership with OMNIA Global to develop over 1 GW of storage projects in Europe over two years, starting with a 50 MW/75 MWh project in Sweden set for 2026. NVVE shares were up 0.8% premarket to about $0.93.
