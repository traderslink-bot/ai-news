Summary

Scinai Immunotherapeutics Ltd. announced a second amendment to the PinCell S.r.l. option agreement and a revised €12 million non-dilutive FENG SMART Path grant application to advance PC111, a fully human anti-sFasL monoclonal antibody, through human proof of concept for pemphigus vulgaris and Stevens–Johnson Syndrome/Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis. The grant would fund up to 80% of a €15 million integrated R&D program. The option conditions extend to 31 August 2026, with the exercise period through 30 September 2026. The revised SMART Path submission features enhanced project design and translational scope intended to generate non-clinical and First-in-Human data to support progression decisions. A potential €12 million grant would enable early clinical development while preserving balance sheet flexibility and minimizing shareholder dilution. No approved therapies currently exist for SJS/TEN, and management notes potential accelerated regulatory pathways may apply contingent on results.
