Summary

Scinai Immunotherapeutics Ltd. announces a second amendment to its binding option agreement to acquire PinCell S.r.l. and a revised €12 million non-dilutive FENG grant application to fund an integrated €15 million R&D program for PC111, a fully human monoclonal antibody targeting soluble Fas Ligand (sFasL) for pemphigus vulgaris and SJS/TEN. The funding would be 80% co-financed; the option conditions and exercise periods have been extended to Aug 31, 2026 and Sept 30, 2026, respectively. A decision on the grant is expected in 3–4 months after submission. The program aims to advance early clinical development and human proof of concept while preserving balance sheet flexibility and minimizing shareholder dilution.

Positives
  • Secures €12 million of non-dilutive funding for the PC111 program
  • 80% project co-financing
  • Advances PC111 toward human proof of concept
  • Alignment of option timeline with grant evaluation process
  • Strengthened SMART Path application with translational design
  • Potentially eligible for accelerated regulatory pathways for PV and SJS/TEN
  • Preserves balance sheet flexibility and minimizes shareholder dilution
Negatives
  • Grant approval is not guaranteed and could be awarded for a lower amount or with conditions
  • Grant review and disbursement could be delayed
  • If grant funding is not obtained, alternative financing would be needed
  • Clinical and regulatory development risks inherent to PC111
  • Regulatory and IP risks and ongoing listing requirements
