Summary

Bionano Genomics announced Day 1 highlights from its 2026 Symposium, focusing on advances in optical genome mapping (OGM) for hematologic malignancies. The virtual session featured 9 presentations from academic centers in Europe and the U.S., with more than 1,000 attendees from 70 countries. OGM demonstrated high concordance with traditional cytogenetics and the potential to enhance detection of structural variants when used with NGS in VIA software. Live panel discussions addressed implementation, validation, and future roles of OGM in research. Day 2 topics include new oncology and bioprocessing applications. Recordings will be available on YouTube; attendance for the event is free. Bionano emphasizes its mission to transform genome analysis, while noting products are for research use only and not diagnostic procedures. The release also includes the standard forward-looking statements disclaimer and financial risk factors typical of corporate communications.
