Summary

Artelo Biosciences, Inc. (ARTL) reported 2025 year-end results: R&D expenses of $5.4 million, G&A expenses of $6.0 million, net loss of $12.9 million ($12.52 per share); cash and investments of $0.6 million as of December 31, 2025. Key clinical updates include positive Phase 1 SAD results for ART26.12 with plans to initiate MAD in Q3 2026; positive interim Phase 2 CAReS results for ART27.13 showing weight and activity improvements with a favorable safety profile; favorable UK MHRA guidance for ART12.11 enabling planned Phase 1 studies with potential accelerated pathways. The company plans to initiate first-in-human studies with an oral solid dosage form for ART12.11 in early 2027, contingent on toxicology results. A European Patent Office Notice of Allowance covers ART27.13, extending protection through December 2041.

Positives
  • Phase 1 SAD for ART26.12 showed a favorable safety profile, predictable pharmacokinetics, and dosing flexibility.
  • Plan to open enrollment to MAD for ART26.12 in Q3 2026.
  • Interim Phase 2 CAReS data for ART27.13 showed improvements in body weight, lean body mass, and physical activity with a favorable side-effect profile vs placebo.
  • UK MHRA provided favorable regulatory guidance supporting Phase 1 plans for ART12.11 and potential accelerated pathways.
  • Notice of Allowance from the European Patent Office for ART27.13 extending patent protection to Dec 2041.
  • Plan to initiate first-in-human studies with an oral solid dosage form for ART12.11 in early 2027.
Negatives
  • Net loss of $12.9 million for the year ended December 31, 2025, vs $9.8 million prior year.
  • Cash and investments totaled only $0.6 million as of December 31, 2025, indicating limited near-term liquidity.
  • General and administrative expenses increased to $6.0 million in 2025 from $4.1 million in 2024.
  • Reliance on future toxicology results and regulatory approvals for ongoing and future clinical programs.
