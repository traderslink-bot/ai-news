Summary

Franklin Street Properties Corp. announced it closed a $320 million secured credit facility with an affiliate of TPG Credit, repaying $248.9 million of existing indebtedness with an initial draw of $258.5 million (net of a $16.5 million original issue discount). The facility allows up to $45 million of delayed draw term loans to fund tenant improvements, leasing commissions, building improvements and other approved uses. The aggregate facility matures February 26, 2029, with a potential one-year extension, and carries an initial coupon of 9.0% and an exit fee of 4% of the funded amount upon repayment. Collateral is a first-priority lien on substantially all assets. Alter Domus (US) LLC will act as administrative agent. The company said refinancing near-term debt maturities provides flexibility, while it continues reviewing strategic alternatives to maximize shareholder value, and expects to focus on property-level initiatives in a challenging office market.

Positives
  • Provides $320 million secured financing to refinance near-term debt.

  • Maturity extended to 2029 with potential one-year extension.
  • First-priority lien on substantially all assets improves collateral position.
  • TPG Credit acts as lender, forming a strategic financing relationship.
Negatives
  • Initial coupon rate of 9.0% increases annual interest expense.
  • Exit fee of 4% of the funded amount due upon repayment.
  • Delayed Draw terms are subject to certain conditions; extension option also subject to conditions.
  • Strategic alternatives review is ongoing with uncertain outcomes.
