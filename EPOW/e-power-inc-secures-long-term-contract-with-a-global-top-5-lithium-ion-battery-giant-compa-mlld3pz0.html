Summary

E-Power Inc. (NASDAQ: EPOW) announced it has secured a long-term anode material supply contract with a global Top-5 lithium-ion battery manufacturer and that the parties have reached strategic intent to co-invest in a 30,000-tonne-per-year anode material facility in Indonesia. The company highlights that selection by the Partner reflects its competitive cost structure and product reliability. E-Power also notes its joint venture is building a separate 50,000-ton capacity graphite anode plant in Guizhou, China; no financial terms, timelines, or binding definitive agreement details for the Indonesia plant were disclosed.

Positives
  • Secured long-term anode material supply contract with a Global Top-5 lithium-ion battery manufacturer.
  • Strategic intent to co-invest in a 30,000-tonne/year anode material plant in Indonesia, indicating potential volume growth and localization.
  • Company's selection cited as validation of competitive cost structure and product reliability.
  • Existing joint venture constructing a 50,000-ton capacity graphite anode plant in Guizhou, China, which may support scaling.
Negatives
  • Article provides no financial terms, contract volumes, pricing, or expected revenue impact.
  • Indonesia facility is described as a strategic intent to co-invest (not a finalized, binding agreement).
  • Forward-looking statements and risks noted; outcomes depend on qualification, market demand, regulations and other factors.
