Summary

Azitra, Inc. announced that The University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center has been added as a clinical site for its Phase 1/2 ATR-04 trial (NCT06830863) evaluating a topical live biotherapeutic for EGFR inhibitor–associated rash. MD Anderson becomes the sixth clinical site, potentially accelerating enrollment and milestones for ATR-04 (ATR04-484), a live biotherapeutic product engineered from Staphylococcus epidermidis. The multicenter, randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled study is designed to assess safety and tolerability with early efficacy signals, with Cohort 1 currently enrolling eight patients. ATR-04 has FDA Fast Track designation for treating EGFRi-associated rash, which affects up to about 150,000 patients in the U.S.; results from this early-stage trial are not yet available.

Positives
  • MD Anderson Cancer Center added as a clinical site, expanding the trial’s footprint to six sites.
  • ATR-04 has FDA Fast Track designation for EGFR inhibitor–associated rash.
  • Phase 1/2 trial design is multicenter, randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled.
  • Enrollment for Cohort 1 is ongoing, with eight patients planned for this cohort.
  • AZTR provides a mechanistic rationale for ATR-04, including potential to reduce inflammatory markers and Staphylococcus aureus presence.
Negatives
  • The program is in early-stage development with no reported clinical results yet.
  • Cohort 1 comprises a small number of patients (eight), limiting early efficacy conclusions.
  • Outcomes depend on ongoing enrollment, regulatory progression, and trial results, which carry inherent clinical and development risks.
