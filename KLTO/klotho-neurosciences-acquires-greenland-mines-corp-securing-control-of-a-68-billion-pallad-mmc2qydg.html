Summary

Press release: Klotho Neurosciences, Inc. (Nasdaq: KLTO) announced the acquisition of Greenland Mines Corp., which controls an 80% interest and an option to acquire the remaining 20% of the Skaergaard Project in Southeast Greenland. The definitive merger agreement was entered March 3, 2026, and closed March 4, 2026. Consideration is payable in convertible preferred stock, and on an as-converted basis Greenland Mines is expected to own approximately 93% of fully diluted shares outstanding; conversion is subject to shareholder approval. The transaction is expected to be treated as a reverse merger for accounting purposes. Klotho will rebrand (name and ticker change planned for late March 2026) and operate two divisions: Natural Resources (Skaergaard development) and Cell & Gene Therapy. Skaergaard hosts a 2022 NI 43-101 Indicated and Inferred Mineral Resource of 25.4 Moz palladium-equivalent and 23.5 Moz gold-equivalent (364.37 million tonnes at 2.17 g/t PdEq) with a cited gross in-situ metal value of approximately $68 billion based on February 2026 metal prices. The project is permitted for exploration with ongoing environmental and metallurgical studies. Forward-looking statement and technical disclaimers noted.
