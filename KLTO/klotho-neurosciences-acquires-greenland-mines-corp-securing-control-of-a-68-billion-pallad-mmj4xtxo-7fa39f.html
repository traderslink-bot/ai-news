Summary

Klotho Neurosciences, Inc. announced the acquisition of Greenland Mines Corp., giving Klotho an 80% interest in the Skaergaard Project with an option to acquire the remaining 20%. Upon closing, Klotho's ownership on a fully diluted basis is expected to be about 93%. The Skaergaard Project, located in Southeast Greenland, is described as a large undeveloped palladium, gold and platinum deposit with an estimated gross in-situ resource value of approximately $68 billion as of February 2026. The transaction will be treated as a reverse merger for accounting purposes, and the company plans a name and stock ticker change to Greenland Mines in late March 2026. The company will operate two divisions: Natural Resources (Skaergaard exploration and development) and Cell and Gene Therapy (KLTO-202 for ALS). Closing is targeted for March 4, 2026, with leadership changes and a new board member planned.
