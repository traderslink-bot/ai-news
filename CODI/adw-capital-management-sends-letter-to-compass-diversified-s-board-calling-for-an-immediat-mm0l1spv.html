Summary

ADW Capital Management, which beneficially owns approximately 7.65% of Compass Diversified (NYSE: CODI), issued an open letter urging CODI's board to immediately commence a formal strategic review with the objective of an orderly liquidation. ADW argues the company's persistent structural discount to NAV is driven by a misaligned external management services agreement (Compass Group Management LLC) that pays ~2.0% of "Adjusted Net Assets," incentivizing fee-generating capital growth over per-share value. ADW cites alleged management failures including a fraud at subsidiary Lugano, heavy dilution (shares outstanding rose from ~19.5M at IPO to ~75.2M), substantial fees (management fees ~$74.8M in 2024) and corporate/subsidiary debt (approx. $923M inter-company debt at subsidiary level and $894M additional net parent debt). ADW estimates potential liquidation value in excess of $26.00 per share versus the recent share price of $7.79, demands termination of the management services agreement, and says it is prepared to pursue all available actions to protect shareholder interests.
