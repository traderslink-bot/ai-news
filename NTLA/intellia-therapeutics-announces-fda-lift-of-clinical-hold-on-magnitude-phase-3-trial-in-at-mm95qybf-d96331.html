Summary

FDA has removed the IND clinical hold for Intellia's MAGNITUDE Phase 3 trial of nexiguran ziclumeran (nex-z) in ATTR-CM. Mitigation measures include enhanced liver monitoring, guidance for short-term steroid use after dosing if liver enzymes rise, and exclusion criteria for certain liver abnormalities. For MAGNITUDE, additional exclusions include a recent history of cardiovascular instability and an ejection fraction below 25% at screening. MAGNITUDE is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in about 1,200 patients; MAGNITUDE-2 is a similar trial in about 60 patients with hereditary ATTR amyloidosis with polyneuropathy. Nex-z targets the TTR gene using CRISPR/Cas9 and has Orphan Drug and RMAT designations in the U.S. and Orphan Drug designation in the EU; Intellia collaborates with Regeneron.

Positives
  • FDA removed the clinical hold on the IND for MAGNITUDE
  • Alignment with the FDA on mitigation measures to resume enrollment
  • Implementation of enhanced liver monitoring and short-term steroid guidance
  • Additional exclusion criteria to improve patient safety (liver abnormalities; cardiovascular criteria)
  • MAGNITUDE and MAGNITUDE-2 enrollment expected to resume
  • Nex-z has potential to be a one-time treatment for ATTR-CM and ATTRv-PN; CRISPR-based approach; collaboration with Regeneron
Negatives
  • Initial clinical hold was prompted by Grade 4 liver transaminases and increased bilirubin in a dosed patient
  • Reintroduction includes exclusion criteria that may restrict eligible patients (liver abnormalities, CV instability history, EF < 25%)
