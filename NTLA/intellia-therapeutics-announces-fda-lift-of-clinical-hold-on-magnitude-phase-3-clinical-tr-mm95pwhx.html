Summary

Press release: Intellia Therapeutics (NASDAQ: NTLA) announced the U.S. FDA has removed the clinical hold on the IND for MAGNITUDE, its Phase 3 trial of nexiguran ziclumeran (nex-z, aka NTLA-2001) in transthyretin amyloidosis with cardiomyopathy (ATTR-CM). The company and FDA agreed on mitigation measures — enhanced liver monitoring, guidance for short-term steroid treatment if early transaminase elevations occur, exclusion of patients with certain liver abnormalities, and additional exclusions for recent cardiovascular instability and EF <25% — to allow resumption of enrollment. The original holds on MAGNITUDE and MAGNITUDE-2 were imposed on Oct 29, 2025 after a patient in MAGNITUDE experienced Grade 4 liver transaminase elevations and increased total bilirubin. MAGNITUDE is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial of ~1,200 patients (2:1 randomization; single 55 mg infusion) with a composite cardiovascular endpoint; MAGNITUDE-2 (hereditary ATTR with polyneuropathy) is ~60 patients (1:1). Intellia noted the MAGNITUDE-2 hold was resolved in January and is working with investigators, ethics committees and regulators to resume enrollment. Forward-looking statements and collaboration with Regeneron are disclosed.
