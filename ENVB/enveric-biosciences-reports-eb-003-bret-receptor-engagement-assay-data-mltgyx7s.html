Summary

Enveric Biosciences (NASDAQ: ENVB) reported proprietary BRET assay data showing its lead candidate EB-003 engages both Gq- and β-arrestin–mediated signaling downstream of the 5-HT2A receptor, with a modest bias toward β-arrestin versus serotonin. The company says both pathways are engaged at biologically relevant levels, is planning further testing (including Gi pathway assessment), and continues to advance EB-003 through IND-enabling studies as a potential non-hallucinogenic neuroplastogen for antidepressant and anxiolytic effects.

Positives
  • Proprietary BRET assays developed and validated in-house to characterize 5-HT2A pathway signaling
  • EB-003 demonstrates biologically relevant engagement of both Gq and β-arrestin signaling pathways
  • EB-003 shows modest preference toward β-arrestin over Gq relative to serotonin
  • Findings align with independent academic research suggesting mechanistic separation between therapeutic effects and hallucinations
  • Company is advancing EB-003 through IND-enabling studies
Negatives
  • EB-003 remains in preclinical/IND-enabling stage and has not entered clinical trials
  • Further testing required, including assessment of the Gi pathway to evaluate hallucinogenic potential
  • Press release contains forward-looking statements and lists typical development, regulatory, funding, and clinical risks
