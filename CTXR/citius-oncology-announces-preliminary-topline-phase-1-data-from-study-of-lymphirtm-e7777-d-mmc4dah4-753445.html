Summary

Topline results from an investigator‑initiated Phase 1 trial of LYMPHIR (E7777) given before CD19‑directed CAR‑T therapy in high‑risk relapsed/refractory DLBCL show an 86% overall response rate at one month (57% CR, 29% PR), with 1‑year PFS of 77% and 1‑year OS of 84%. LYMPHIR depleted regulatory T cells, and no dose‑limiting toxicities were observed up to 9 μg/kg. TEEs included Grade 1–2 capillary leak syndrome, fever, and transient liver enzyme elevations; CRS occurred in 43% (Grade 1–2) and ICANS in 21% (mostly low grade). All 14 patients proceeded to CAR‑T therapy. The trial was open‑label, dose‑escalation and not powered to assess efficacy.

Positives
  • Oral overall response rate (ORR) of 86% at one month (CR 57%, PR 29%).
  • One-year progression-free survival (PFS) of 77%.
  • One-year overall survival (OS) of 84%.
  • LYMPHIR achieved depletion of circulating regulatory T cells in all but one patient.
  • No dose‑limiting toxicities observed up to 9 μg/kg; LYMPHIR was generally well tolerated.
  • All 14 patients completed LYMPHIR and proceeded to commercial CAR‑T therapy.
  • Data supports potential enhancement of CAR‑T efficacy when LYMPHIR is used to modulate Tregs.
Negatives
  • Phase 1 trial not designed or powered to evaluate efficacy definitively.
  • Small sample size (n=14).
  • CRS occurred in 43% and ICANS in 21% (both primarily Grade 1–2), indicating manageable but non-negligible toxicity.
  • Investigator‑initiated study; results require confirmation in larger trials.
  • Results pertain to a high‑risk DLBCL cohort with features like double/triple hit genetics, which may limit generalizability.
