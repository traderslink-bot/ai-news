Summary

Topline results from an investigator-initiated Phase 1 trial evaluating LYMPHIR in combination with pembrolizumab for relapsed or refractory gynecologic cancers (ovarian and endometrial). In 25 evaluable patients, the study reported a 24% objective response rate (ORR) and a 48% clinical benefit rate (CBR) among 21 evaluable patients, with no unexpected safety signals or serious immune-related adverse events observed at any dose level. The trial aimed to establish a recommended LYMPHIR dose for a Phase 2 study; full results will be presented at an international cancer conference later this year. LYMPHIR use in this study was investigational and outside its FDA-approved indication.

Positives
  • 24% objective response rate (ORR) among evaluable patients
  • 48% clinical benefit rate (CBR) among evaluable patients
  • No unexpected safety signals or serious immune-related adverse events observed at any dose level
  • Goal to establish a recommended LYMPHIR dose for Phase 2
  • Study led by University of Pittsburgh/UPMC Magee-Women's Hospital
  • Full results to be presented at an international cancer conference later this year
Negatives
  • Phase 1 study not designed or powered to evaluate efficacy or long-term outcomes
  • Small sample size (25 evaluable patients; 21 evaluable for efficacy)
  • LYMPHIR use in the study was investigational and outside FDA-approved indication
