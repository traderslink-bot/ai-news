Summary

VisionWave to acquire 51% controlling stake in C.M. Composite Materials Ltd. for 250,000 VisionWave common shares; closing conditions and regulatory approvals required, expected to close in Q1 2026. The transaction follows a $10 million QSpeed development SOW. C.M. reported approximately $17.3 million revenue and about $3.0 million net income before tax for fiscal year 2025 (IFRS, unaudited). Post-closing, VisionWave will consolidate C.M.’s results. Independent valuation by BDO Consulting Group valued C.M. at $50 million. Due diligence conducted over 64 days at C.M. facilities. The deal envisions integrating QSpeed software with certified aerospace manufacturing. Potential expansion into India discussed, with several regulatory and market risks noted. There are significant integration and regulatory risks, and no assurance of realized benefits.

Positives
  • Binding definitive agreement to acquire 51% controlling stake in C.M. Composite Materials Ltd., a supplier to Iron Dome and Barak 8 programs
  • Consideration is 250,000 VisionWave common shares for the 51% stake
  • Independent valuation by BDO Consulting Group supports a $50 million valuation for C.M.
  • Previously announced $10 million QSpeed development SOW with milestone-based revenue potential in 2026
  • Expected closing in the first quarter of 2026 and subject to regulatory approvals
  • Potential strategic synergy from combining QSpeed software with a certified aerospace manufacturing base
Negatives
  • Closing is not guaranteed and depends on customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals

  • QSpeed platform remains under development and its application to C.M.'s manufacturing is unproven
