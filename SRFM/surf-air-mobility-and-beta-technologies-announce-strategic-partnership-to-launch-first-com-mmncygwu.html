Summary

Press release: Surf Air Mobility (NYSE: SRFM) signed a firm Aircraft Purchase Agreement for 25 BETA Technologies (NYSE: BETA) all-electric ALIA CTOL aircraft, with options for up to 75 additional aircraft. Surf Air will be the launch operator for BETA's passenger ALIA in Hawaii, initially deploying ALIA aircraft for cargo services under Mokulele Airlines and later commercial passenger Part 135 scheduled and on-demand service after certification. Surf Air plans to establish BETA factory-authorized MRO service centers with geographic exclusivity in launch regions (starting with Hawaii), operate and deploy BETA charging/ground infrastructure, and conduct demonstration flights in 2026. The companies cite expected operating cost reductions and potential new revenue from MRO and integrated services. The release also notes forward-looking risks related to development, certification, financing, regulatory and supplier dependencies.
