Summary

Press release announcing "Uber Air powered by Joby": Uber will integrate Joby Aviation's all-electric air taxi into the Uber app with one-tap booking and end-to-end trip connections (including Uber Black). Joby expects to carry first passengers in Dubai later this year. Joby's four-passenger, piloted aircraft uses six tilting propellers, can reach speeds up to 200 mph and ranges up to 100 miles; Joby reports more than 50,000 miles of flight tests and is in the final stage of FAA certification required for U.S. commercial operations. The release reiterates prior strategic moves: Joby's 2021 acquisition of Uber's Elevate division and acquisition of Blade's passenger business, with Blade service planned to appear in the Uber app in 2026. Target markets include Dubai, New York, Los Angeles, the U.K., and Japan.
