Summary

Northern Dynasty Minerals Ltd. (TSX: NDM; NYSE: NAK) and its wholly owned U.S. subsidiary Pebble Limited Partnership announced the U.S. Department of Justice filed its brief in Alaska Federal Court on February 17, 2026 in connection with the companies' summary judgment matter. CEO Ron Thiessen criticized the EPA for defending the prior administration's veto and said the company is reviewing the DOJ filing with counsel and will issue detailed comments in the coming days. The release reiterates that Pebble Partnership holds a 100% interest in 1,840 mineral claims comprising the Pebble deposit and includes standard forward-looking and risk disclosures about permitting, legal challenges, financing and project viability.

Positives
  • DOJ filed a brief in the Alaska Federal Court on Feb 17, 2026 (concrete procedural development).
  • Company provided a timely public update and said it is reviewing the filing with legal counsel.
  • Pebble Partnership remains 100% owned by Northern Dynasty and the company reaffirmed the asset position (1,840 mineral claims).
Negatives
  • DOJ filing supports the EPA's defense of the prior administration's Final Determination, per the company statement.
  • Significant permitting and legal risk remains; the company states securing required federal and state permits is not guaranteed.
  • Project development depends on multiple contingent factors (feasibility, partner support, financing), per the company's forward‑looking risk disclosures.
