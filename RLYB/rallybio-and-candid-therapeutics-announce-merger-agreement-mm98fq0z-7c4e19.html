Summary

Rallybio will acquire Candid Therapeutics through a merger, with the combined company to operate as Candid Therapeutics, Inc. and trade on Nasdaq under the ticker CDRX. The Financing for over $505 million is oversubscribed and upsized, providing a pro forma cash balance at closing to fund operations through 2030 and support Candid's T-cell engager pipeline through multiple milestones, including Phase 2 readouts for cizutamig. The merger is expected to close in mid-2026, subject to stockholder approvals, SEC registration, Hart-Scott-Rodino clearance, and other customary closing conditions. Post-closing, Rallybio equityholders are expected to own about 3.65% and Candid equityholders about 96.35% (assuming Rallybio has about $37.5 million in net cash). The combined company will have a pro forma cash position around $700 million and will host a joint conference call on March 2, 2026 at 8:30 AM ET.

Positives
  • Oversubscribed and upsized financing of over $505 million committed by a syndicate of leading healthcare institutional investors and mutual funds.
  • Pro forma cash balance at closing expected to fund operations through 2030.
  • Combined company to have a strong financial foundation to advance Candid's diversified TCE pipeline through multiple milestones, including Phase 2 readouts for cizutamig.
  • The combined company will operate as Candid Therapeutics, Inc. and trade on Nasdaq under the ticker CDRX.
  • Candid's lead program cizutamig (BCMA TCE) has Phase 2 development planned in 2026 with favorable tolerability and potential best-in-class profile.
  • Additional Candid programs include CND261 (CD20 TCE) and CND319 (CD19/CD20 TCE) with early data and planned first-in-human studies in mid-2026.
  • Pro forma cash position around $700 million at closing to support pipeline advancement.
  • Leadership for the combined company will be led by Dr. Ken Song (CEO of Candid) with an experienced management team.
  • Post-closing ownership is expected to be ~3.65% Rallybio equityholders and ~96.35% Candid equityholders (on a treasury stock method basis).
  • Contingent value rights (CVRs) will be issued to Rallybio pre-closing stockholders.
  • Boards of both companies have unanimously approved the transaction.
  • Joint conference call and webcast scheduled for March 2, 2026 at 8:30 AM ET.
Negatives
  • Closing is subject to stockholder approvals, effectiveness of a registration statement with the SEC, and the expiration of the Hart-Scott-Rodino waiting period.
  • Timing and completion of the Merger depend on satisfaction of customary closing conditions, which may not be met.</
  • The exchange ratio determining post-closing ownership could lead to higher or lower ownership for Rallybio stockholders than currently anticipated.
  • The Financing is subject to completion and may not be consummated, potentially impacting the transaction.
  • Uncertainties inherent in clinical development and regulatory approvals could affect the value and timing of pipeline milestones.
  • There is no guarantee that CVRs will be redeemable or pay proceeds to Rallybio stockholders.
