Summary

Agroz Inc. (NASDAQ: AGRZ) received a deficiency letter from Nasdaq's Listing Qualifications Department stating its Ordinary Shares closed below $1.00 for 30 consecutive business days, triggering a 180-day period to regain compliance by August 17, 2026. To regain compliance, the closing bid price must be at least $1.00 for 10 consecutive trading days. If not cured, an extension of up to 180 additional calendar days may be available if the company meets continued listing requirements and provides notice of its intention to cure, potentially via a reverse stock split. If the deficiency cannot be cured, Nasdaq may delist the shares, subject to appeal rights. The company plans to monitor the bid price and consider options to regain compliance.

Positives
  • No immediate effect on Nasdaq listing
  • Initial 180-day period granted to regain compliance (until Aug 17, 2026)
  • Potential extension of up to 180 days if criteria are met and cure plan is put in place
  • Regaining compliance can be achieved by maintaining a $1.00 bid price for 10 consecutive trading days
Negatives
  • Closing bid price below $1.00 for 30 consecutive business days
  • Risk of delisting if unable to cure within the compliance period
  • Potential requirement for a reverse stock split to regain compliance during an extended period
