Summary

Paramount Skydance amended its $30.00 per-share all-cash tender offer for Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD), adding a $0.25 per-share quarterly 'ticking fee' for delays after Dec 31, 2026, agreeing to fund the $2.8bn termination fee payable to Netflix, and offering to backstop/fully reimburse up to $1.5bn of WBD's potential debt-exchange costs if needed. The deal is fully financed by $43.6bn of equity commitments (Ellison Family and RedBird) and $54.0bn of debt commitments (Bank of America, Citigroup, Apollo), with a $43.3bn irrevocable personal guarantee from Larry Ellison. Paramount certified compliance with the DOJ second request on Feb 9, 2026 and secured German clearance on Jan 27, 2026; the tender offer expiration was extended to March 2, 2026 and 42,345,815 shares had been validly tendered as of Feb 9, 2026. Paramount intends to solicit proxies against the Netflix transaction and emphasizes its offer’s greater cash certainty versus the Netflix proposal.

Positives
  • All-cash $30.00 per share offer provides fixed, certain cash consideration to WBD shareholders
  • Adds $0.25 per-share quarterly ticking fee for delays beyond Dec 31, 2026 to compensate for closing slippage
  • Paramount will fund $2.8bn termination fee to Netflix, eliminating that liability for WBD shareholders
  • Fully financed commitments: $43.6bn equity and $54.0bn debt commitments backing the offer
  • Irrevocable personal guarantee of $43.3bn from Larry Ellison on the equity financing
  • Paramount certified compliance with DOJ second request (Feb 9, 2026) and secured German foreign investment clearance (Jan 27, 2026)
Negatives
  • Transaction remains subject to regulatory approvals and shareholder acceptance
  • Paramount acknowledges forward-looking risks and other uncertainties that could prevent the tender offer or a negotiated merger from closing
  • WBD accelerated timetable and ongoing competing Netflix transaction could complicate shareholder decision-making
