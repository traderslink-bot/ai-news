Summary

LiqTech International reported FY2025 revenue of $16.5 million, up 13% year over year; Q4 2025 revenue was $3.1 million, down 8% from Q4 2024. FY2025 net loss was $8.6 million, improved from $10.3 million in 2024; Adjusted EBITDA was $(5.0) million. Ending cash was $5.1 million. The company provided a 2026 revenue outlook of $23–$27 million and aims for positive Adjusted EBITDA in the mid-to-high range of that guidance. Operational highlights include 49% growth in total systems and aftermarket revenue to $8.2 million, a record 34 pool systems sold (24 delivered in 2025, 10 in early 2026), two commercial water-for-energy/industrial system deployments, a China JV for marine water treatment, and several 2025 orders that underpin growth.

Positives
  • FY2025 revenue rose 13% to $16.5 million.
  • Gross profit for 2025 was $1.25 million with a gross margin of 7.6%, up from $0.25 million and 1.7% in 2024.
  • Ending cash balance was $5.1 million as of December 31, 2025.
  • Total systems and aftermarket revenue increased 49% in 2025 to $8.2 million.
  • Record pool system sales: 34 pool systems sold in 2025 (24 delivered in 2025; 10 scheduled for early 2026).
  • Two commercial systems delivered for water for energy and industrial applications in 2025.
  • Three marine dual-fuel engine water treatment system orders in 2025; five backlog for 2026.
  • Expansion in the U.S. with a Texas service center; joint venture in China for marine water treatment.
  • 2025 EBITDA metrics shown as improvements; guidance for 2026 revenue growth and potential positive Adjusted EBITDA.
Negatives
  • Q4 2025 revenue declined 8% year over year to $3.1 million (from $3.4 million in Q4 2024).
  • FY2025 net loss of $8.6 million, compared to $10.3 million in 2024.
  • Adjusted EBITDA for 2025 was $(5.0) million, compared to $(6.1) million in 2024.
  • Manufacturing capacity underutilization continued to affect profitability.
  • Inventory review led to adjustments for obsolescence and slow-moving items.
  • The company fell short of its original revenue guidance due to delays with a large oil and gas order, which remains active in the pipeline and is expected to contribute in 2026.
  • Linear costs associated with the newly formed joint venture in China and related selling/distribution activities increased expenses.
