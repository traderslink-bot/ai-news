Summary

Bionano Genomics announced highlights from the final day of Symposium 2026, including updates from laboratories on scaling optical genome mapping (OGM) with automation and multiple OGM systems, and the VIA software for data analysis. The Ionic system for DNA and RNA purification via isotachophoresis was discussed, along with UHMW DNA isolation and digital karyotyping concepts. The event drew about 1,250 registrants and 35 outside speakers across 33 presentations and 50 posters. Lab examples noted included Radboud UMC and CHU de Québec. Winners of the poster competition were announced. Session recordings will be available on YouTube, and executives discussed adoption, scalability, and cost considerations. Bionano’s products are stated for research use only.

Positives
  • OGM scalability with automation and multiple systems
  • VIA software described as a unified analytical platform for genomic data
  • Ionic system for DNA/RNA purification described as scalable with higher purity nucleic acids
  • Discussion of cost and scalability advantages of OGM over long-read sequencing
  • Laboratory case studies highlighting increased sample throughput (e.g., goal of 3000 samples/year at Radboud UMC)
  • Promotion of collaboration through ICOGM
  • Posters and talks showcased breadth of applications and advancements
