Summary

Cadrenal Therapeutics reports expansion of its 12-LOX inhibitor platform following the acquisition of selective inhibitors. Phase 2 data for CAD-1005 in heparin-induced thrombocytopenia show a reduction in thrombotic events; an End-of-Phase 2 meeting with the FDA is scheduled for later this month. HIT remains the near-term regulatory priority. The company describes a differentiated platform with potential applications across thrombotic and inflammatory diseases, including multi-billion-dollar markets. CAD-1005 is the first selective 12-LOX inhibitor in clinical development, and the portfolio includes oral candidates; regulatory designations include FDA Orphan Drug and Fast Track, and EMA orphan designation. The pipeline also features tecarfarin and frunexian.

Positives
