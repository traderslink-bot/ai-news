Summary

BioCardia reported late-breaking echocardiography results from its Phase III CardiAMP HF Trial in ischemic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF). Autologous CardiAMP cell therapy showed decreased pathological left ventricular remodeling over time versus controls, with endpoints including reduced fatal and non-fatal major adverse cardiovascular events and improved quality of life. Echocardiography assessments by the Yale Cardiovascular Research Group found non-significant changes in left ventricular end-diastolic volume (p=0.06) and end-systolic volume (p=0.09) in the overall population, while patients with elevated NTproBNP showed clinically meaningful and statistically significant reductions (p=0.02 and p=0.01). The results were presented by Dr. Amish Raval at THT on behalf of CardiAMP HF Investigators. BioCardia notes CardiAMP has FDA Breakthrough designation and is investigational under U.S. law; therapy is delivered via catheter-based autologous cells and is reimbursed by CMS where applicable.

Positives
  • Positive evidence of decreased pathological left ventricular remodeling over time with CardiAMP therapy versus control
  • Endpoints include reduced fatal and non-fatal major adverse cardiovascular events and improved quality of life
  • In patients with elevated NTproBNP, differences in LV volumes were clinically meaningful and statistically significant (p=0.02 for EDV, p=0.01 for ESV)
Negatives
  • Overall LV volume changes were not statistically significant (p=0.06 for LVEDV; p=0.09 for LVESV)
  • Therapy described as investigational and limited by U.S. law to investigational use
