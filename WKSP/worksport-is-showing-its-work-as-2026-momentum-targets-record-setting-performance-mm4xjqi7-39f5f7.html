Summary

Worksport reports 2026 momentum with revenue growth, manufacturing partnerships, and certifications. Highlights include a Terravis Energy manufacturing partner for the Aetherlux heat pump with Zerofrost tech; a strategic partnership with Potomac International Partners to pursue federal and commercial markets; Q4 revenue up 65% year-over-year with gross profit up 380%, and full-year revenue up 91% to about $16.2 million with gross margins in the low-30% range. Additional progress includes government evaluation of the Aetherlux Pro heat pump and COR portable energy system certifications, with UL and CSA certifications expected in Q1 2026. Management notes ongoing shipping of clean-energy products, expanding OEM/distribution discussions, and Hyundai's SOLIS Solar cover partnership, all while leveraging the existing tonneau-cover business.

Positives
  • November revenue in December 2025 marked the highest monthly total in company history, establishing a record baseline entering 2026.
  • Q4 revenue grew 65% year over year; gross profit increased 380%.
  • Full-year revenue rose 91% to approximately $16.2 million; gross margins expanded into the low 30% range.
  • Terravis Energy selected a major Asian manufacturing partner for the Aetherlux heat pump with Zerofrost anti-frosting technology, signaling manufacturing readiness.
  • Strategic partnership with Potomac International Partners to accelerate federal government and commercial market engagement for the clean energy ecosystem.
  • Aetherlux Pro heat pump undergoing government evaluation with AHRI and ENERGY STAR certification processes advancing.
  • COR portable energy system received key certifications including transport and federal compliance approvals; final UL and CSA certifications expected in Q1 2026.
  • Clean energy products shipping; OEM and major distribution discussions progressing; growth anticipated in 2026.
  • Hyundai partnership for the SOLIS Solar cover; clean energy extensions are being layered onto existing revenue streams.
