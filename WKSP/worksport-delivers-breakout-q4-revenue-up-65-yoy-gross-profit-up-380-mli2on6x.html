Summary

Worksport Ltd. (NASDAQ: WKSP) reported preliminary, unaudited top-line results for Q4 ended Dec 31, 2025: net sales of $4.84M (+65% YoY), gross margin of 32% (vs. 11% prior year) and gross profit of ~ $1.5M (+380% YoY). Full-year 2025 revenue was a record $16.2M (+91% YoY). Management attributes margin expansion to domestic production at its West Seneca, NY facility, manufacturing efficiencies, and fixed cost absorption that offset ~35% aluminum price increases. The company says R&D for its SOLIS (solar tonneau), COR (portable energy system) and Aetherlux (heat pump/Terravis Energy) products is largely complete and these technologies are entering commercialization, with SOLIS and COR beginning commercial launch in January 2026. Worksport expects to provide FY2026 revenue and cash-flow guidance later this quarter.

Positives
  • Q4 net sales of $4.84M, up 65% year-over-year
  • Q4 gross margin expanded to 32% from 11% a year earlier (2,100 bps improvement)
  • Q4 gross profit approximately $1.5M, up ~380% year-over-year
  • Full-year 2025 revenue $16.2M, up 91% versus 2024
  • Domestic manufacturing scale-up (West Seneca facility) credited with margin improvement and fixed-cost absorption
  • Core clean-energy products (SOLIS, COR) transitioning from R&D to commercialization, with commercial launch begun January 2026
  • Management expects R&D intensity to decline as technologies move to monetization
Negatives
  • Reported results are preliminary and unaudited
  • Significant aluminum input cost increases (~35% in 2025) created cost pressure
  • Commercialization and revenue contribution from new products are early-stage and depend on execution
  • No specific FY2026 revenue or cash-flow guidance provided yet
