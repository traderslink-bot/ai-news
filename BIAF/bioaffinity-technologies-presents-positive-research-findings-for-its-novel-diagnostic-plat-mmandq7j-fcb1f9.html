Summary

Poster presented at the AAAAI 2026 annual meeting in Philadelphia on March 1 reports that bioAffinity Technologies’ diagnostic platform can identify antibody drug receptors in sputum, including receptors for dupilumab and benralizumab, to guide personalized asthma and COPD therapies and monitoring. The company also notes CyPath Lung as a noninvasive lung cancer test that uses flow cytometry and AI, with prior results showing high sensitivity, specificity, and accuracy.

Positives
  • Poster demonstrates ability to identify antibody drug receptors in sputum for dupilumab and benralizumab.
  • Technology aims to guide personalized treatment decisions for asthma and COPD and monitor inflammatory changes over time.
  • Uses a flow cytometry platform with automated AI analysis.
  • CyPath Lung noninvasive test for early lung cancer shows high sensitivity (92%), specificity (87%), and accuracy (88%).
Negatives
  • Forward-looking statements acknowledge risks and uncertainties that could cause actual results to differ, including regulatory clearance, data size, and market factors.
  • Uncertainties related to clinical validation, data availability, sample sizes, and changes in standards of care.
  • Risks related to reimbursement, third-party reliance, manufacturing and supply, and competitive pressures.
