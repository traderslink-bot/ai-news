Summary

Press release: Zeo Energy (NASDAQ: ZEO) announced a non-binding memorandum of understanding with Creekstone Energy to develop approximately 280 MW of baseload generation to support Creekstone's Gigasite AI/data center project in Millard County, Utah. Zeo will perform a pre-feasibility study to design solar-plus-long-duration (thermal/chemical) storage solutions to create firm baseload power, may provide FEED/FEL engineering and project management, and could seek project financing. The release notes Zeo's August 2025 acquisition of Heliogen for long-duration generation expertise and reiterates Creekstone's plans to deliver >300 MW of gas-powered energy in H1 2027 and expand to multi-gigawatt capacity.

Positives
  • MOU to develop ~280 MW of baseload power for a large AI/data center campus in Millard County, Utah
  • Zeo will apply long-duration storage expertise (acquired via Heliogen) to firm intermittent solar into dispatchable baseload power
  • Zeo is conducting a pre-feasibility study and may provide engineering (FEED/FEL) and project management services
  • Potential for Zeo to obtain project financing and expand into the high-growth AI/data center power market
Negatives
  • The MOU is non-binding and does not obligate either party to proceed to a definitive agreement
  • Project timelines and outcomes are uncertain (Creekstone's 2027 plans may not materialize as announced)
  • Forward-looking statements include multiple risk factors such as financing, operational execution, regulatory and listing risks, and limited liquidity of Zeo's securities
