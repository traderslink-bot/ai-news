Summary

Market News Updates projects the global generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) treatment market to grow from about $1.8 billion in 2023 to more than $4.26 billion by 2033, with the broader anxiety-treatment market potentially exceeding $12 billion to $17+ billion over the next decade. Treatments include traditional medications, psychotherapy, and digital/telehealth tools increasing access. Helus Pharma reported Phase 2 topline results for HLP004 as an adjunct to standard care in adults with moderate-to-severe GAD, showing a 10.4-point reduction in HAM-A at six weeks (p<0.0001) and 67% responders with 39% remitters at six months. In-clinic treatment duration is described as about three hours with acute effects lasting ~90 minutes, and the regimen was well tolerated with no drug-related serious adverse events. Additional updates cover Acadia Healthcare, Johnson & Johnson, BrainsWay, and Firefly Neuroscience. The release notes potential future data for HLP003 in major depressive disorder in Q4 2026 and includes standard investment-disclaimer language.

Positives
  • Global GAD treatment market projected to reach $4.26 billion by 2033, ~9% annual growth
  • Helus Pharma HLP004 Phase 2 results show clinically meaningful efficacy when added to standard care
  • HLP004 achieved a 10.4-point HAM-A reduction at six weeks (p<0.0001)
  • At six months, 67% of treated patients were responders and 39% were in remission
  • In-clinic treatment duration around three hours with acute effects lasting ~90 minutes
  • Digital mental health tools and telehealth improving access to care
  • Broader anxiety-treatment market expected to exceed $12–17+ billion in the next decade
  • Potential future data release for HLP003 in major depressive disorder in Q4 2026
Negatives
  • Phase 2 topline data based on 36 patients; small sample size; results preliminary
  • No new monotherapy approved for GAD in almost two decades, indicating ongoing unmet need and development risk
