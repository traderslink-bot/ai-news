Summary

Skye Bioscience reported Q4 and full-year 2025 results and provided a business update. The company initiated the CBeyond Expansion Study (Part C) with higher-dose nimacimab IV cohorts (400 mg and 600 mg) and received FDA Type C meeting minutes to inform Phase 2b design and a potential add-on development path with incretin therapy. In combination with semaglutide, nimacimab showed 22.3% weight loss at 52 weeks with a favorable safety profile; 26-week data indicated ~3% incremental weight loss and improved body composition versus semaglutide alone. Off-treatment weight regain favored the combination (17.8% vs 37.3%), with the combination maintaining fat loss and gaining lean mass. Manufacturing and formulation updates, including ENHANZE licensing and higher-concentration nimacimab, support Phase 2b readiness. As of December 31, 2025, cash and equivalents plus short-term investments totaled $25.7 million, with projected funding through Q4 2026 to support nimacimab trials and early manufacturing. Upcoming milestones include Q2 2026 preclinical data presentations and a Q4 2026 topline for CBeyond Phase 2a Expansion Study and Phase 2b design finalization.

Positives
  • CBeyond Expansion Study (Part C) initiated with higher-dose nimacimab IV cohorts (400 mg and 600 mg).
  • FDA Type C meeting minutes received to inform Phase 2b design and potential add-on with incretin therapy.
  • Nimacimab plus semaglutide achieved 22.3% weight loss at 52 weeks with favorable safety (no CNS toxicity; no increase in GI or neuropsychiatric adverse events).
  • At 26 weeks, combination showed ~3% incremental weight loss versus semaglutide alone with improvements in lean-to-fat mass ratio and waist circumference; no plateau observed.
  • 13-week off-treatment weight regain favored the combination (17.8%) vs semaglutide alone (37.3%), with maintained fat loss and lean mass gains.
  • APC (antibody-peptide conjugate) program showed preclinical proof-of-concept data, indicating potential for broader metabolic applications.
  • Manufacturing readiness progress includes ENHANZE licensing for higher-volume SC administration and ongoing Phase 2b preparation (drug substance/product manufacturing) with cost-of-goods optimization and supply-chain strengthening.
  • Higher-concentration nimacimab formulation development and exploration of monthly dosing to improve patient experience and commercial attractiveness.
Negatives
  • Net loss for the year ended December 31, 2025 was $55.9 million.
  • Research and development expenses for 2025 rose to $42.4 million, driven by contracted clinical study and manufacturing costs for planned Phase 2b trials.
  • Cash and cash equivalents at December 31, 2025 were $5.9 million, indicating a reduced near-term liquidity position relative to prior year.
