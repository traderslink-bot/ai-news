Summary

Highway Holdings Limited (NASDAQ: HIHO) reported fiscal year 2026 third quarter and nine months results ended December 31, 2025. For the first nine months, net revenue declined 34.6% to $3.874 million from $5.925 million a year earlier, and net loss was $0.427 million ($0.09 per basic share) versus net income of $0.421 million ($0.10 per diluted share) a year ago. For the third quarter, net revenue declined 40.5% to $1.147 million from $1.929 million, and net loss was $0.115 million ($0.02 per basic share) versus net income of $0.092 million ($0.02 per diluted share) in the prior year. Gross margins were 25.5% in Q3 and 28.9% for the first nine months, down from 34.7% and 36.5% respectively. The company cited the loss of a significant motor business customer and ongoing actions to diversify and pursue acquisitions as factors. It noted a reversal of tax provisions contributing to after-tax results (Q3 $327k; nine months $488k). Cash and cash equivalents were about $5.1 million as of December 31, 2025, with a current ratio of 4.26:1 and minimal debt. The company is pursuing a potential acquisition of Regent-Feinbau Adermann GmbH and plans a major reorganization to reduce operating expenses. It also discussed restarting production of a game console line in China and potential new business in Myanmar involving import licenses for electric car kits, though the venture is not guaranteed. Management expects near-term results to reflect the transition but believes a return to profitability could occur in fiscal year 2027.

Positives
  • Strong liquidity with approximately $5.1 million in cash and cash equivalents and a current ratio of 4.26:1; minimal debt.
  • Chinese factory operating smoothly and positioned as a production contributor.
  • Tax provision reversals added to after-tax results (Q3: $327k; nine months: $488k).
  • Strategic focus on diversification, cost reduction, and acquisitions to stabilize and grow the business.
  • Restart of game console manufacturing with first shipments anticipated following February production start.
Negatives
  • Net revenue down 34.6% for the first nine months to $3.874 million; third quarter revenue down 40.5% to $1.147 million.
  • Nine-month net loss of $0.427 million vs. $0.421 million net income a year ago; third-quarter net loss of $0.115 million vs. $0.092 million net income a year ago.
  • Gross margin declined: Q3 25.5% vs 34.7% year-ago; nine months 28.9% vs 36.5% year-ago.
  • Significant motor business customer loss in Myanmar impacting revenue and operations.
  • Ongoing uncertainty around obtaining Myanmar import license and rights to assemble electric car kits; no guarantee of success.
  • Chinese factory volume not yet at level to fully cover company expenses.
  • Plan for major reorganization and cost reductions indicate ongoing near-term restructuring needs.
  • Exposure to currency fluctuations (RMB and Kyat) without hedging could affect results.
