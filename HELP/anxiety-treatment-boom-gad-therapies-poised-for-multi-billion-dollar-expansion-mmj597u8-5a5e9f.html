Summary

Projected global GAD therapy market to reach $4.26 billion by 2033, with the broader anxiety-treatment market potentially exceeding $12–$17 billion in the next decade (approx. 9% annual growth). Helus Pharma reports Phase 2 topline results for HLP004 showing a 10.4-point HAM-A reduction at six weeks (p<0.0001) with 67% responders and 39% remitters at six months in 36 patients, adjunct to standard care. In-clinic treatment duration is around three hours with ~90-minute acute effects and no drug-related serious adverse events reported. Helus also plans to release HLP003 data for MDD in Q4 2026. Additional company updates include Acadia Healthcare, Johnson & Johnson, BrainsWay, and Firefly Neuroscience activity in the space.

Positives
  • Market forecast: GAD therapies projected to reach $4.26B by 2033, with broader anxiety treatment market potentially >$12B to $17B in the next decade.
  • Phase 2 topline results for HLP004 show a clinically meaningful HAM-A reduction of ~10.4 points at six weeks (p<0.0001) when added to standard of care.
  • Six-month data indicate 67% responders and 39% remitters in the pooled population.
  • In-clinic treatment timeline is short (about three hours) with acute effects lasting ~90 minutes, suggesting scalability for interventional psychiatry settings.
  • No drug-related serious adverse events reported in the Phase 2 evaluation.
  • HLP003 data in MDD expected in Q4 2026, indicating ongoing development pipeline.
  • Multiple other biotech and healthcare companies in the space are noted for ongoing research and development.
Negatives
  • Phase 2 trial involved 36 patients, which may limit generalizability and requires confirmation in larger studies.
  • Results are adjunctive to standard antidepressant/anteriolytic therapy, not monotherapy.
  • Six-week and six-month outcomes are reported; long-term efficacy beyond one year is not detailed.
  • The article includes forward-looking statements and disclosures typical of press releases, which may not reflect confirmed results.
