Summary

Press release from Market News Updates highlighting projected growth in generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) treatments (market ~ $1.8B in 2023 to an estimated $4.26B by 2033, ~9% CAGR) and reporting clinical and commercial developments. Key item: Helus Pharma (NASDAQ: HELP) announced topline Phase 2 signal-detection results for HLP004 as adjunctive therapy in 36 moderate-to-severe GAD patients randomized 2:1 to 20 mg or 2 mg intramuscular doses given twice three weeks apart. Primary results: mean HAM-A reduction of 10.4 points at six weeks (p<0.0001) for the 20 mg arm vs baseline; pooled population showed 67% responders and 39% in remission at six months; 20 mg arm: 59% response / 32% remission at week 6; 2 mg arm: 30% response/remission at week 6. Safety/tolerability: no drug-related serious adverse events reported; acute effects ~90 minutes with discharge readiness ~3 hours. Other company mentions: Acadia (NASDAQ: ACHC) reported Q4 2025 revenue $821.5M and a net loss per diluted share of $(13.02) driven by a $996.2M goodwill impairment; Johnson & Johnson (NYSE: JNJ), BrainsWay (NASDAQ: BWAY) and Firefly Neuroscience (NASDAQ: AIFF) updates referenced. Disclosure: MNU was paid $4,600 to distribute the Helus-related release and republished company-provided material; forward-looking statements and typical risk disclaimers included.
