Summary

Kosmos Energy (KOS) agreed to sell its 40.375% non-operating working interest in the Ceiba Field and Okume Complex (Block G, Equatorial Guinea) to Panoro Energy for $180.0 million upfront (subject to adjustments) plus contingent payments up to $39.5 million (including $12.5M linked to Ceiba production and $9M payable in each of 2027, 2028 and 2029 subject to oil price and production thresholds). Panoro will acquire the Kosmos subsidiary that holds the Block G interest. The transaction has an effective date of January 1, 2025, has Government of Equatorial Guinea approval, is expected to close mid-2026, and remains subject to CEMAC customary approval. Kosmos will use proceeds to reduce borrowings under its reserves-based lending facility, expects roughly $100 million of combined capex and G&A savings over two years post-close, and will retain upside via contingent payments.
