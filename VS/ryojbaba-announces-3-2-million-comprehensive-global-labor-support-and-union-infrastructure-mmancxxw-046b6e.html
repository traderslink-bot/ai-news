Summary

rYojbaba Co., Ltd. announced a one-year, ¥500 million (USD $3.2 million) agreement with the International Labor Union to provide comprehensive international labor support and union infrastructure development, including services to strengthen ILU's sustainability, expand membership, coordinate with international institutions, and build a foreign worker-led membership structure. Revenue will be recognized as performance obligations are satisfied; as of December 31, 2025, no revenue related to the agreement has been recognized. rYojbaba operates labor consulting and health services, including 29 osteopathic clinics and 1 beauty salon in Japan.

Positives
  • One-year, ¥500 million (USD 3.2 million) agreement with ILU
  • Potential to scale international labor support and union infrastructure
  • Partnerships and relationships with United Nations agencies and other institutions (ILO, UNFPA, UNU)
  • Plan to develop a wage improvement model linked to public subsidy programs
  • Global reach and advisory capabilities to support ILU's initiatives
Negatives
  • Risks related to execution, performance, enforceability, modification or termination of the Agreement
  • Uncertainty around timing and recognition of revenue under the Agreement
  • Dependence on public subsidy programs; availability and continuation risks
  • Potential changes in labor, immigration, or employment laws in Japan or other jurisdictions
  • Reliance on third-party organizations and international institutions
  • Geopolitical and macroeconomic risks; foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations
  • Reputational risks
