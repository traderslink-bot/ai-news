Summary

Gossamer Bio reports topline PROSERA Phase 3 results for seralutinib in PAH. At Week 24, placebo-adjusted 6-minute walk distance (6MWD) improved by +13.3 meters (p=0.0320) but did not meet the prespecified alpha threshold of 0.025. Median 6MWD changed by +28.2 meters with seralutinib vs +13.5 meters with placebo. In the prespecified intermediate- and high-risk subgroup (n=234), placebo-adjusted 6MWD improved by +20.0 meters (p=0.0207) with three of four key secondary endpoints achieving p<0.0125. NT-proBNP at Week 24 favored seralutinib (location shift -120.4 ng/L; p=0.0002). Safety showed TEAEs in 86.5% vs 80.5% and SAEs in 16.0% vs 18.9%; transaminases elevations ≥3x ULN occurred in 13% vs 1%. A conference call and FDA discussions on next steps are planned; SERANATA enrollment is paused to evaluate PROSERA results.

Positives
  • Prespecified intermediate- and high-risk subgroup (n=234) showed a placebo-adjusted 6MWD improvement of +20.0 meters (p=0.0207) across endpoints
  • NT-proBNP at Week 24 favored seralutinib with a significant location shift (-120.4 ng/L; p=0.0002)
  • Three of four key secondary endpoints favored seralutinib in the overall population (p<0.0125 for several endpoints)
  • Safety profile: comparable TEAEs between groups and lower SAE rate with seralutinib (16.0% vs 18.9%)
  • CTD-APAH subgroup showed a notable placebo-adjusted 6MWD gain (+37.0 meters; p=0.0104)
  • Plans to meet with the FDA to discuss next development steps
  • Company hosting a conference call and webcast to discuss topline results
Negatives
  • Primary endpoint narrowly missed the prespecified alpha (p=0.0320 vs α=0.025); therefore, p-values for key secondary endpoints cannot be evaluated for statistical significance
  • All p-values reported are nominal; lack of formal statistical significance for the primary endpoint
  • SERANATA enrollment paused to evaluate PROSERA results, introducing uncertainty about the trial program moving forward
