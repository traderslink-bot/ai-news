Summary

Lifeward Ltd. (LFWD) will establish dedicated clinic days at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's Chicago campus starting in Q2 2026 to evaluate and trial the ReWalk Personal Exoskeleton for individuals with spinal cord injury. Shirley Ryan AbilityLab will lead clinical evaluation, training and support. The ReWalk Personal Exoskeleton is FDA-cleared and enables standing and walking, including negotiating stairs and curbs. The collaboration aims to streamline the pathway from evaluation to real-world use and expand access, potentially improving ambulation, health outcomes and quality of life.

Positives
  • Expands access to the ReWalk Personal Exoskeleton via dedicated clinic days for spinal cord injury patients.
  • Collaboration with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, a leading rehabilitation hospital.
  • Streamlines the pathway from evaluation to real-world use of the ReWalk system.
  • ReWalk is FDA-cleared wearable robotic system suitable for standing and walking, including stairs and curbs.
  • Potential improvements in ambulation, health outcomes and quality of life for users.
Negatives
  • Uncertainty around acceptance of the ReWalk Personal Exoskeleton by healthcare professionals and patients.
  • Regulatory approval timing and processes for future developments.
  • Potential challenges in securing third-party payor reimbursement (e.g., CMS).
  • Manufacturing transitions or supply chain dependencies and related operational risks.
  • Forward-looking statements are subject to uncertainty and other factors.
