Summary

Press release: Astellas and Vir Biotechnology entered a global collaboration to co-develop and co-commercialize VIR-5500, a PRO-XTEN dual-masked PSMA-targeting CD3 T-cell engager currently in Phase 1 for advanced/metastatic prostate cancer. Vir will receive $335M in upfront and near-term payments (including $240M cash, $75M equity at a 50% premium, and a $20M milestone). Global development costs will be shared (Astellas 60% / Vir 40%). Astellas will lead U.S. commercialization with Vir retaining an option to co-promote and U.S. profit/loss split 50/50; Astellas will have exclusive ex-U.S. commercialization rights. Vir is eligible for up to $1.37B in development, regulatory and sales milestones plus tiered double-digit royalties on ex-U.S. net sales. Closing is subject to customary conditions including HSR clearance. Vir will present updated Phase 1 data and host a conference call.
