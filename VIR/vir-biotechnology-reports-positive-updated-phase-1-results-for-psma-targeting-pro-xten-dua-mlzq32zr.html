Summary

Press release: Vir Biotechnology reported updated Phase 1 dose-escalation data for VIR-5500 in metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (n=58; data cut-off Jan 9, 2026). VIR-5500 monotherapy showed a favorable safety profile with no dose-limiting toxicities observed; Grade ≥3 treatment-related adverse events occurred in 12% (7/58). Cytokine release syndrome was reported in 50% (29/58) of patients, generally Grade 1. Dose-dependent anti-tumor activity was seen in the highest dosing cohorts (≥3,000 µg/kg Q3W; n=22), with PSA50 declines in 82% (14/17) and PSA90 declines in 53% (9/17) of PSA-evaluable patients, and RECIST-evaluable objective responses in 45% (5/11) (four confirmed). Reductions on PSMA-PET and tumor shrinkage including visceral lesions were reported. Company plans monotherapy and combination dose-expansion cohorts in Q2 2026 and intends to initiate a registrational trial in 2027; data to be presented at ASCO GU 2026.
