Summary

Solana Company (NASDAQ: HSDT) announced a tri-party custody model with Anchorage Digital (collateral manager) and Kamino (on-chain lending) that enables institutions to borrow against natively staked SOL while keeping assets in segregated accounts at Anchorage Digital Bank. The structure allows borrowers to continue earning native staking rewards (~7% cited) while accessing on-chain liquidity, using Anchorage's Atlas collateral management for 24/7 automated LTV oversight, margin movements, and rules-based liquidations. Solana Company positions the model as a repeatable blueprint to bring institutional capital to Solana's DeFi ecosystem; HSDT remains focused on accumulating SOL as its principal business objective.

Positives
  • First-of-its-kind tri-party custody model enabling borrowing against natively staked SOL
  • Anchorage Digital to act as collateral manager with assets held in borrowers' segregated accounts at Anchorage Digital Bank
  • Institutions can earn staking rewards while unlocking on-chain liquidity
  • 24/7 automated collateral management (Atlas) for LTV oversight, margin/collateral movements, and rules-based liquidations
  • Collaboration positioned as a scalable, repeatable blueprint for institutional participation in protocol borrowing
  • Aligns with HSDT's stated mission to accumulate SOL and provide institutional exposure to Solana
Negatives
  • Forward-looking statements warn the collaboration may fail to deliver anticipated benefits
  • Exposure to SOL price volatility could materially affect outcomes
  • Evolving regulatory environment and custodial/regulatory risk noted as potential adverse factors
