Summary

iQSTEL disclosed preliminary FY25 revenue around $317 million, below its $340 million guidance, with a current annual revenue run rate near $400 million and an adjusted EBITDA run rate of about $2.7 million. Management sees a potential profit inflection point as EBITDA could reach $9–$15 million as revenue scales toward $500–$600 million, and plans two acquisitions to push revenue beyond $500 million while expanding into AI-enabled telecom solutions and cybersecurity services. The company aims for seven-digit annual revenue contributions by 2027, and shares traded about 0.5% lower in premarket trading, near a 52-week low.
