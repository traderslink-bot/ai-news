Summary

uniQure reported full-year and fourth-quarter 2025 results and highlighted progress across its gene therapy portfolio. As of December 31, 2025, cash, cash equivalents and current investment securities totaled $622.5 million; 2025 total revenues were $16.1 million and net loss was $198.971 million. R&D expenses were $140.673 million and SG&A expenses were $65.456 million. The company completed enrollment in the first AMT-260 dose cohort; updated AMT-191 Fabry data showed dose-dependent increases in α-Gal A activity with durability. AMT-130 regulatory discussions with the FDA included a Type A meeting; the FDA indicated it cannot deem the Phase I/II data sufficient for marketing approval and recommended a randomized trial; the company plans a Type B meeting in Q2 2026. The company raised $404.2 million in 2025 and refinanced debt, resulting in a cash runway into the second half of 2029.

Positives
  • AMT-130 36-month data show significant slowing of disease progression (cUHDRS 75%; TFC 60%) in high-dose treated patients.
  • Enrollment in the AMT-260 Phase I/IIa first dose cohort completed; second dose cohort started; data expected in Q2 2026.
  • AMT-191 shows dose-dependent increases in α-Gal A activity with durable effects across dose levels.
  • Balance sheet strengthened with $622.5 million in cash and investments as of 12/31/2025; $404.2 million raised in 2025; debt refinancing extended term to 2030; likely funding into 2H 2029.
  • Earnings call planned; ongoing clinical progress across Huntington's disease, MTLE, Fabry and other programs.
Negatives
  • FDA stated it cannot agree that Phase I/II AMT-130 data with an external control are sufficient to support a marketing application; plan to discuss Phase III development and pursue a Type B meeting in 2026.
  • AMT-162 enrollment remains on voluntary pause after IDMC recommendation due to safety signals; one SAE of dose-limiting toxicity reported.
  • Total 2025 revenue declined to $16.1 million from $27.1 million in 2024.
  • Net loss for 2025 was $198.971 million, versus $239.556 million in 2024.
