Summary

Press release reporting uniQure's fourth quarter and full-year 2025 results and program updates. Cash, cash equivalents and current investment securities were $622.5 million as of December 31, 2025, and the company expects funds to carry into the second half of 2029. 2025 revenue was $16.1 million (down from $27.1M in 2024); net loss was $198.97 million, or $3.46 per share. uniQure raised $404.2 million in follow-on offerings in 2025 and refinanced $50 million of debt with optional additional tranches tied to milestones. Clinical updates: AMT-130 (Huntington's disease) reported positive 36-month data for 12 high-dose patients (75% slowing on cUHDRS, p=0.003; 60% slowing on TFC, p=0.033; mean NfL -8.2% at 36 months) but FDA indicated Phase I/II external-control data are insufficient for approval and strongly recommended a prospective randomized, double-blind, sham surgery-controlled study; uniQure plans to request a Type B meeting in Q2 2026. AMT-260 (MTLE) completed first cohort enrollment and showed a 92% seizure reduction in an initial patient; additional data expected in H1 2026. AMT-191 (Fabry) showed dose-dependent, durable α-Gal A increases with some patients stopping ERT; dosing paused in mid/high cohorts pending evaluation of asymptomatic Grade 3 liver enzyme elevations in two patients (dose-limiting). AMT-162 (SOD1 ALS) enrollment remains on voluntary pause after an IDMC recommendation following a DLT SAE. Company will host an investor call March 2, 2026.
