Summary

Press release / market commentary arguing rare-earth magnets are strategic to U.S. defense and industry. It highlights REalloys Inc. (ALOY) as an operational U.S. downstream rare-earth metallization and alloying facility in Euclid, Ohio that claims to convert heavy rare earth oxides into magnet-grade metals and alloys, supply DoD programs, and avoid offshore processing. Key commercial arrangements cited: a commercial processing / offtake agreement with the Saskatchewan Research Council (SRC) securing 80% of upgraded facility output; non-binding LOIs/agreements for feedstock from AltynGroup (Kazakhstan), St George Mining (Brazil/Araxá) for up to 40% subject to definitive agreements, and a LOI for up to 15% from Greenland's Tanbreez project. REalloys plans ~US$21 million expansion to increase heavy rare earth and NdPr capacity (design outputs cited: ~30 t dysprosium oxide, 15 t terbium oxide, 400 t NdPr metal rising to 600 t/yr) with first production expected early 2027. The article also references Microsoft (MSFT), NVIDIA (NVDA), Alphabet (GOOGL), Tesla (TSLA), General Motors (GM) and Caterpillar (CAT) in the broader rare-earth/technology context. The piece includes publisher disclosures that the owner of OilPrice.com owns shares in the featured company and that some statements are forward-looking.
