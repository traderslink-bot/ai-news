Summary

Genprex announces Japanese Patent Office grant of a patent covering Reqorsa gene therapy with PD-L1 antibodies to treat cancer and European Patent Office grant for Reqorsa with PD-1 antibodies to treat cancer. Genprex holds exclusive licenses for these patents, expanding protection across key markets and supporting the Acclaim-3 ES-SCLC trial (Reqorsa + Tecentriq). Acclaim-3 is a Phase 1/2 maintenance study with an interim analysis planned after the 25th patient in 2026 and first-half-2026 enrollment for the initial cohort. The release notes FDA Fast Track and Orphan Drug Designations for Acclaim-3 and outlines broader IP coverage including US, Japan, Europe, Korea, and other regions.

Positives
  • Japanese Patent Office grants a patent covering Reqorsa gene therapy in combination with PD-L1 antibodies to treat cancer.
  • European Patent Office grants a patent covering Reqorsa in combination with PD-1 antibodies to treat cancer.
  • Genprex has exclusive licenses to these patents, strengthening its global IP position.
  • Patents provide protection for therapeutic combinations that include the Acclaim-3 clinical trial (Reqorsa + PD-L1 antibodies).
  • Existing IP framework includes granted patents in multiple regions (US, Korea) and coverage across additional markets (Japan, Europe, etc.).
  • Acclaim-3 benefits from FDA Fast Track Designation and Orphan Drug Designation.
  • News aligns with ongoing expansion of Genprex’s IP portfolio in key markets.
Negatives
  • Patent grants do not guarantee commercial success or regulatory approval for Reqorsa or its combinations.
  • Interim analysis and enrollment progress are contingent on trial execution and patient recruitment; results are not provided in the release.
