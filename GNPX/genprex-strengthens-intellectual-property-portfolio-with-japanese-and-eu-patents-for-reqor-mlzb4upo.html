Summary

Genprex announces that the Japanese Patent Office granted a patent for Reqorsa® Gene Therapy used with PD-L1 antibodies to treat cancer and the European Patent Office granted a patent for Reqorsa® in combination with PD-1 antibodies. Genprex holds exclusive licenses to these patents, expanding its IP protection in key markets and supporting the Acclaim-3 ES-SCLC trial (Reqorsa® with Tecentriq). Acclaim-3 is a Phase 1/2 study with FDA Fast Track and Orphan Drug Designations; interim analysis is planned for 2026. The release includes standard forward-looking statements caveats.

Positives
  • Patents granted in Japan and Europe for Reqorsa in combination with PD-L1/PD-1 antibodies, strengthening global IP protection.
  • Genprex maintains exclusive licenses to these patents.
  • Patents cover therapeutic combinations including the Acclaim-3 clinical trial context.
  • Acclaim-3 has FDA Fast Track Designation and Orphan Drug Designation.
  • IP protection spans US, Japan, Europe and other listed jurisdictions, broadening potential competitive barriers.
Negatives
  • Forward-looking statements contain risks and uncertainties; actual outcomes may differ.
  • Interim analysis timing and results for Acclaim-3 are not guaranteed and depend on enrollment and other factors.
  • Patents may be challenged, invalidated, or have limited commercial impact despite grants.
