Summary

Yelp reported full-year 2025 results: record net revenue of $1.465 billion (up 4% YoY), net income of $146 million (up 10% YoY), adjusted EBITDA of $369 million (up 3% YoY; 25% margin), and diluted EPS of $2.24 (up 19% YoY). Services advertising revenue was a record $948 million (up 8% YoY) while Restaurants, Retail & Other advertising declined 6%. Total paying advertising locations fell 3% but average revenue per location reached an annual record; ad clicks declined 7% and average cost per click rose 10%. Yelp generated $323.7 million of free cash flow in 2025 and repurchased ~$291 million of stock. The company announced an agreement with OpenAI, completed the acquisition of Hatch, and plans to invest in an AI transformation. 2026 guidance: net revenue $1.455–1.475 billion and adjusted EBITDA $310–330 million. Cash and cash equivalents were $216 million at year-end.

Positives
  • Record net revenue of $1.465 billion in 2025 (up 4% YoY)
  • Net income increased 10% to $146 million; diluted EPS $2.24 (up 19%)
  • Adjusted EBITDA $369 million (25% margin), up 3% YoY
  • Services advertising revenue reached a record $948 million (up 8% YoY)
  • Strong free cash flow of $323.7 million for 2025
  • Strategic AI moves: acquisition of Hatch and agreement with OpenAI
Negatives
  • Paying advertising locations declined 3% year over year
  • Restaurants, Retail & Other advertising revenue down 6% YoY
  • Ad clicks declined 7% YoY, indicating weaker consumer demand
  • 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance ($310–330M) is materially below 2025 adjusted EBITDA ($369M)
  • Significant share repurchases (~$291M) during 2025 reduced cash resources
