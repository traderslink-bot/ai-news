Summary

Press release: Fermi America (Nasdaq & LSE: FRMI) announced deepened strategic partnership with Hyundai Engineering & Construction (Hyundai E&C). The companies are advancing Front-End Engineering Design (FEED) work supporting four Westinghouse AP1000 reactors planned for Project Matador, an 11 GW private energy campus outside Amarillo, Texas. Fermi participated in Hyundai E&C's Large-Scale Nuclear Technology Seminar to engage contractors, strengthen the U.S. nuclear supply chain and workforce readiness, and align potential EPC execution. Leadership emphasized the need to accelerate large-scale power infrastructure for AI and industrial growth. The release includes forward-looking statements and notes ongoing FEED activities (site layout, cooling evaluations, cost and schedule development) but no announced EPC contract or construction start date.

Positives
  • Strategic partnership with Hyundai E&C, a company that has built 24 reactors globally
  • Ongoing FEED work for four AP1000 units supporting Project Matador
  • Project Matador planned as an 11 GW private energy campus to supply baseload power for AI, data centers and industry
  • Active industry engagement (seminar) to mobilize contractors and strengthen U.S. nuclear supply chain and workforce
Negatives
  • Project currently in FEED stage; no EPC contract, construction start, or financing commitments disclosed
  • Plans rely on external support (mentions DOE and DOC) and contain forward-looking statements
  • Large-scale U.S. nuclear construction has been largely stalled for decades, signaling potential execution and regulatory challenges
