Summary

Cyngn announced a commercial contract to deploy its DriveMod Tugger at WEG's Bluffton, Indiana manufacturing facility to automate material transport between machining and warehouse areas, replacing manual forklift runs and supporting up to 60 pallets per day with a typical payback period of under two years.

Positives
  • Supports reallocation of forklift labor from routine material transport to higher-value production and warehouse tasks.
  • Reduces on-floor congestion and non-value-added driving by automating repetitive transport.
  • DriveMod Tugger handles pallet movement with throughput up to 60 pallets per day.
  • Payback period reportedly less than 2 years.
  • Demonstrates adoption of autonomous material transport technology by a large manufacturing customer.

  • None
