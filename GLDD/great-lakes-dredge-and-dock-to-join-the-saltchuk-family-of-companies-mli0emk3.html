Summary

Great Lakes Dredge & Dock Corporation (NASDAQ: GLDD) and Saltchuk Resources announced a definitive agreement for Saltchuk to acquire Great Lakes in a cash tender offer of $17.00 per share, implying an aggregate equity value of ~ $1.2 billion and total transaction value of $1.5 billion. The offer represents a 25% premium to Great Lakes' 90-day VWAP as of Feb. 10, 2026 and a 5% premium to the company's all-time high; the board unanimously approved the transaction and recommends stockholders tender. The deal is expected to close in Q2 2026, subject to customary conditions including HSR clearance and majority tender, and is supported by fully committed financing from Bank of America, Wells Fargo, U.S. Bank and PNC. Upon closing Great Lakes will operate as a standalone business within Saltchuk and its common stock will be delisted from Nasdaq.

Positives
  • Cash consideration of $17.00 per share provides a 25% premium to 90-day VWAP and 5% to all-time high
  • Board of Directors unanimously approved and recommends tendering shares
  • Transaction supported by fully committed financing (Bank of America, Wells Fargo, U.S. Bank, PNC)
  • Great Lakes to operate as a standalone business within Saltchuk, preserving operations and brand
Negatives
  • Transaction requires expiration of HSR waiting period and tender of a majority of outstanding shares to close
  • Deal will result in Nasdaq delisting of Great Lakes common stock
  • Completion risks include regulatory approvals, potential competing offers, legal challenges and employee retention concerns
  • Transaction subject to customary closing conditions and forward-looking risks described by the companies
