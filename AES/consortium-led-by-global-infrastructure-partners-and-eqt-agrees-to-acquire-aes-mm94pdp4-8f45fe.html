Summary

An investment consortium led by Global Infrastructure Partners and EQT agrees to acquire AES for $15.00 per share in cash, valuing AES at about $10.7 billion of equity and an enterprise value of approximately $33.4 billion, including assumed debt. The deal represents a 40.3% premium to the 30-day VWAP prior to July 8, 2025. AES would become a private company; AES Indiana and AES Ohio would remain locally operated regulated utilities. The transaction is expected to close in late 2026 or early 2027, subject to stockholder and regulatory approvals. The consortium will fund 100% of the purchase price with equity; dividends are expected to continue through closing. The arrangement is intended to provide AES with greater capital flexibility to fund growth beyond 2027 and expand its clean energy platform across the Americas. Fairness opinions were provided by J.P. Morgan Securities LLC and Wells Fargo Securities LLC, with CalPERS and QIA as co-underwriters.

Positives
  • Cash consideration of $15.00 per AES share provides immediate liquidity to stockholders.
  • Premium of 40.3% to the 30-day VWAP prior to July 8, 2025.
  • AES gains access to capital to fund growth beyond 2027 as a private company.
  • AES Indiana and AES Ohio will continue to be locally operated regulated utilities.
  • Consortium includes established infrastructure investors (GIP and EQT) plus long-term capital providers (CalPERS and QIA).
  • Positioning of AES as a leading clean energy platform across the Americas.
Negatives
  • Transaction requires stockholder and regulatory approvals; closing is not guaranteed and timing is uncertain.
  • Risks and uncertainties associated with a major corporate transaction, including potential litigation and disruption to operations.
  • AES will become a private company, which may entail changes in governance and financing dynamics post-closing (not detailed in the release).
  • The deal may involve significant transaction costs and financing considerations.
