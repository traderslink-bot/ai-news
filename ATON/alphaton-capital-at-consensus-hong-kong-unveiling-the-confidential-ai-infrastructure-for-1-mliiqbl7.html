Summary

AlphaTON Capital Corp. (NASDAQ: ATON) announced its presentations and private sessions at Consensus Hong Kong (Feb 11–12, 2026) to showcase its development of confidential AI and privacy-preserving infrastructure intended for Telegram's ~1 billion monthly users. Public appearances include Anthony Scaramucci and CEO Brittany Kaiser discussing institutional blockchain/AI adoption and AlphaTON's Cocoon AI GPU launch partnership. AlphaTON and Midnight Foundation will present a private podcast on co-developing confidential AI infrastructure for Telegram. The release positions these initiatives as creating scalable, recurring revenue opportunities and first-mover advantage in confidential AI, and states conference content will be posted to the company’s investor relations channels. The release contains standard forward-looking statement disclosures and lists risks including dependence on third parties, financing needs, and uncertainty around legacy business operations.

Positives
  • Public visibility at Consensus Hong Kong with named speakers (Anthony Scaramucci, Brittany Kaiser).
  • Partnership with Midnight Foundation to co-develop confidential AI infrastructure for Telegram.
  • Positioned to serve a large potential addressable market (Telegram’s ~1 billion monthly users).
  • Cocoon AI GPU launch partnership could support confidential compute use cases and monetization through platform-level AI/privacy services.
  • Company will make conference content available to investors via IR channels.
Negatives
  • All strategic and financial benefits are presented as forward-looking and subject to execution risk.
  • Dependence on Telegram’s platform and ecosystem — risks if Telegram’s strategy or adoption changes.
  • Reliance on third-party partners and technology (e.g., Midnight Foundation, GPU partners).
  • Possible financing and operational risks noted, including uncertainty around legacy business and investments (e.g., TON).
  • Company is incorporated in the British Virgin Islands, which may raise governance or jurisdictional considerations for some investors.
