Summary

NexGel Inc. (NXGL) will acquire Celularity's regenerative biomaterial portfolio, adding six commercial-stage products focused on tendon repair, skin grafts, and bone growth. NexGel expects 2026 revenue to reach about $35 million, nearly triple 2025 levels, and to become profitable immediately. The deal includes six products with established insurance reimbursement coverage, and a pipeline with three additional 510(k) filings planned for 2026, 2027, and 2028. Financing of about $1.8 million has been announced previously, with an expected additional approximately $14.9 million in financing to close in Q1 or early Q2 2026. The closing is subject to due diligence.

Positives
  • Revenue projection of about $35 million in 2026 (nearly tripling annual revenue)
  • Expected immediate profitability
  • Six commercial-stage products with established demand and insurance reimbursement pathways
  • Established insurance reimbursement coverage for the portfolio
  • Three additional 510(k) filings planned for 2026-2028
  • Potential to scale biomaterials and hydrogel technologies across multiple sectors
Negatives
  • Financing with approximately $14.9 million expected to close in Q1 or early Q2 2026, contingent on due diligence and closing conditions
  • Financing previously announced of about $1.8 million; not guaranteed
  • Deal closing subject to due diligence and other contingencies
