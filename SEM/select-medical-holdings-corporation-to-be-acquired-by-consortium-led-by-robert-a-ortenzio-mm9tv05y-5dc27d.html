Summary

Select Medical Holdings Corporation entered into a definitive merger agreement with a consortium led by Robert A. Ortenzio, Martin F. Jackson and Welsh, Carson, Anderson & Stowe (WCAS) to acquire all outstanding shares not already owned by the consortium for $16.50 per share in cash, valuing the transaction at approximately $3.9 billion. The Board, on the recommendation of a Special Committee, unanimously approved the merger. The consortium will roll over its equity, and management may roll over as well. The merger is not subject to a financing condition and is expected to close in mid-2026, subject to stockholder and regulatory approvals and other closing conditions. Upon closing, Select Medical will become privately held and deregistered from the NYSE. Financing arrangements are being arranged, with J.P. Morgan and Wells Fargo as lead arrangers and bookrunners; Goldman Sachs advised the Special Committee; various legal advisers are engaged.

Positives
  • Cash offer of $16.50 per share represents a premium to recent trading levels (approximately 18% vs. the unaffected price on Nov 24, 2025 and about 25% vs. the 90-day VWAP).
  • Board unanimously approved the merger following recommendation by the Special Committee.
  • Initial equity rollover by Ortenzio, Jackson and affiliates; potential for continued management continuity via rollover participation.
  • Merger not subject to a financing condition, with debt financing arrangements in place or arranged.
  • Expected close targeted for mid-2026, subject to standard regulatory and stockholder approvals and other closing conditions.
  • Company will continue to operate normally during merger process; current management expected to remain post-closing.
  • Rollover participants collectively own a meaningful stake and have agreed to vote in favor of adoption.
  • Advisors and financing partners are named (J.P. Morgan, Wells Fargo, Goldman Sachs, etc.).
Negatives
  • Requires approval of the holders of the outstanding shares not owned by the Consortium and regulatory clearances (including antitrust reviews).
  • Possible termination of the merger and related termination fees under certain circumstances.
  • CLOSING remains contingent on regulatory approvals and stockholder votes; there is execution risk if conditions are not met.
  • Post-merger integration and transition risks could impact operations and performance.
  • Forward-looking statements and risks outlined in the merger materials may affect outcomes.
