Summary

MOIP issued a Notice of Allowance for a patent covering Anixa's breast cancer vaccine technology. The patent, titled 'Vaccine Adjuvants and Formulations,' is exclusively licensed from Cleveland Clinic and provides composition of matter protection for Anixa's breast cancer treatment and prevention approach in South Korea. The patent complements U.S. and other jurisdictions' patents and advances potential future regulatory approvals and commercialization outside the United States. In a Phase 1 Cleveland Clinic trial, the vaccine met major primary endpoints, was safe and well tolerated, and elicited immune responses in 74% of participants. The vaccine targets human α-lactalbumin, a protein associated with lactation that is aberrantly expressed in certain breast cancers; there are currently no approved vaccines to prevent breast cancer.

Positives
  • Expands international intellectual property coverage for the breast cancer vaccine technology
  • Exclusive license from Cleveland Clinic strengthens collaboration and IP position
  • Phase 1 results: safe and well tolerated with immune responses in 74% of participants
  • Potential pathway toward regulatory approvals and commercialization outside the United States
Negatives
  • No breast cancer vaccine has been approved yet; regulatory approvals required
  • Phase 1 results are early-stage and may not predict later-stage efficacy
